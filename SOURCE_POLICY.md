# Source Policy: Verification & Citation Standards

## 1. Objective

This policy governs the verification, citation, and source-boundary standards for Space-Based-Solar-Power.com.

The objective is to maintain source-disciplined institutional credibility. Every technical metric, economic estimate, physical efficiency baseline, policy claim, program status, and strategic tool input must be tied to a verifiable source or clearly marked as conceptual interpretation.

## 2. Source Credibility Hierarchy

All data models, reference pages, and strategic tools must draw from a controlled source hierarchy.

### Tier 1 — Institutional and Sovereign Sources

Preferred sources include official publications from:

- NASA;
- ESA;
- JAXA;
- national space agencies;
- government energy ministries;
- formal public-sector research bodies;
- recognized intergovernmental institutions.

### Tier 2 — Peer-Reviewed Academic Sources

Accepted academic sources include research published by recognized journals, conferences, and technical publishers, including fields such as:

- space systems engineering;
- wireless power transmission;
- microwave engineering;
- orbital infrastructure;
- energy systems modeling;
- grid integration;
- space economics.

### Tier 3 — Enterprise and Program Disclosures

Enterprise disclosures may be used only when they come from identifiable organizations and are treated as company claims, not neutral facts.

Company announcements, pitch materials, and press releases must not be used as sole evidence for technical feasibility, economic viability, or commercial readiness.

## 3. Claim Classification

Every important claim must be classified as one of the following:

- source-verified fact;
- institutional summary;
- technical explanation;
- strategic interpretation;
- company claim;
- unresolved scenario;
- speculative future pathway.

Speculative claims must never be presented as established fact.

## 4. Mandatory Reference Footprint

Every major reference page must include a final section titled:

`Verified References`

References must include, where available:

`Agency/Author | Year | Title | Publisher/Institution | DOI or Direct URL`

## 5. Constraint Presentation Mandate

If a credible source identifies a major technical, economic, regulatory, safety, or infrastructure constraint, the asset must not hide or downplay it.

Constraints must be presented alongside the proposed capability.

The asset’s credibility depends on explaining both:

- what SBSP could enable;
- what still prevents SBSP from becoming fully mature infrastructure.

## 6. Strategic Tools Source Rule

No strategic tool may produce a classification, readiness signal, constraint profile, or scenario output unless its logic is documented.

Numerical tools require:

- assumptions;
- source ranges;
- methodology notes;
- version number;
- limitation statement;
- no-prediction disclaimer.

## 7. Prohibited Source Behavior

The project prohibits:

- unsourced technical claims;
- inflated feasibility statements;
- hidden economic assumptions;
- copied marketing language;
- anonymous claims;
- fake citations;
- citation stuffing;
- outdated source reuse without date context;
- treating company press releases as neutral proof.

## 8. Source Review

Sources must be reviewed before publication and re-reviewed when:

- a page is updated;
- a tool changes logic;
- a major institutional report is released;
- a claim becomes outdated;
- a source URL breaks;
- a localized edition modifies technical framing.
