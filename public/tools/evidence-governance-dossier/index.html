Evidence Governance Dossier

Evidence Reference

Strategic Purpose

A reference classification of the SBSP evidence base by source tier, claim category, and verification status. The dossier provides practitioners with a governed inventory of what evidence exists, what tier each evidence type occupies, what that evidence does and does not establish, and how to apply appropriate epistemic discipline when drawing on it for analysis, communications, or policy.

Designed for: researchers, journalists, due-diligence analysts, policy analysts, programme evaluators

Decision value: SBSP evidence is distributed across a wide range of source types with very different levels of rigour, independence, and scope. Without a structured framework for evaluating evidence quality, analysts risk either dismissing credible institutional assessments as mere advocacy or treating engineering projections as demonstrated facts. The dossier provides the classification layer that makes responsible use of the evidence base possible.

Tier 1 — Peer-Reviewed Experimental Results Primary research results published in peer-reviewed academic journals or conference proceedings with documented methodology, reproducible experimental conditions, and independent editorial review. Caltech SSPP MAPLE experiment results published in Nature (2023); JAXA WPT ground demonstration publications in IEEE Transactions; academic research on rectenna efficiency, phased array performance, and space solar cell characterisation. Establishes that the described physical phenomenon occurs under the stated experimental conditions. Provides the most reliable evidence for component-level performance claims. Typically at small scale and under controlled conditions; may not reflect system-level performance; experimental context may differ significantly from operational SBSP environment. Cite for specific, bounded component performance claims. Do not generalise beyond the stated experimental parameters.
Tier 2 — Government-Commissioned Feasibility Studies Independent technical feasibility studies commissioned by government bodies or space agencies, conducted by recognised engineering consultancies or research institutions, with documented methodology and published for public review. Frazer-Nash Consultancy feasibility study (commissioned by UK BEIS, 2020); ESA SOLARIS Concurrent Design Facility assessment; NASA SBSP revisit study (2023). Establishes the institutional conclusion on technical feasibility, cost envelope, and development pathway under stated assumptions. Provides the most comprehensive publicly available system-level analysis. Conclusions are conditional on stated assumptions (particularly cost projections); independent technical studies may reach different conclusions; feasibility conclusions do not constitute deployment commitments. Cite for system-level feasibility framing, cost scenario analysis, and institutional conclusions. Always note the stated assumptions that condition the conclusions.
Tier 3 — Institutional Programme Documentation Official technical documentation published by space agencies, government programmes, or institutional research groups describing their programme objectives, architecture, roadmap, and technical approach. JAXA SSPS conceptual study documentation; ESA SOLARIS programme documentation; NASA SBSP revisit programme reports; AFRL SBSP programme documentation. Establishes the institutional framing, programme structure, and stated technical approach of the issuing organisation. Provides authoritative information on what programmes exist and what they are targeting. Programme documentation reflects the issuing institution's perspective and objectives; may not reflect independent technical assessment; roadmap dates are planning assumptions not commitments; programme existence does not imply programme success. Cite for programme structure, institutional framing, and official stated objectives. Distinguish between programme claims and demonstrated results.
Tier 4 — Industry Technical Assessments Technical assessments, capability statements, and programme descriptions published by industrial actors (aerospace companies, contractors) involved in or interested in SBSP development. Northrop Grumman SSPIDR programme documentation; Airbus SBSP position papers; Thales Alenia Space SBSP communications; Mitsubishi Electric WPT research publications. Establishes that industrial actors have assessed SBSP as a technically credible direction worth pursuing. Technical content reflects genuine engineering capability where published with methodology. Industry assessments serve commercial and advocacy functions alongside technical analysis. Industrial actors have financial interest in programme development. Capability statements may reflect aspirational positions. Cite for evidence of industrial engagement and for technical content where methodology is documented. Note the commercial context of industry-published assessments.
Tier 5 — Institutional Communications and Policy Statements Formal public communications from space agencies, government ministries, and institutional bodies expressing policy positions, strategic priorities, or programme endorsements. Includes press releases, parliamentary statements, and official public communications. ESA Council communications on SOLARIS; UK government statements on space energy initiative; Japanese government SBSP policy statements; EU energy policy communications referencing SBSP. Establishes the institutional policy position and political support for SBSP research or investment. Documents the strategic framing adopted by institutional actors. Policy statements reflect political and strategic considerations as well as technical analysis; they do not substitute for technical evidence; political support does not guarantee technical feasibility or programme continuity. Cite for institutional framing and policy context. Do not use as technical evidence. Distinguish clearly between policy position and technical finding.
Tier 6 — Advocacy, Media, and Unanchored Claims Materials produced by advocacy organisations, media articles without independent technical sourcing, conference presentations without peer review, and claims made without explicit reference to primary evidence. SBSP advocacy communications that omit limiting conditions; media coverage that overclaims demonstration results; unanchored deployment timeline predictions; comparative energy cost claims without stated methodology. Documents the discourse and framing in public communications about SBSP. Useful for understanding how SBSP is being described; not useful as technical evidence. Tier 6 materials typically recirculate Tier 1–5 content, sometimes with inaccurate or inflated framing. They introduce the risk of claim laundering — where a Tier 5 institutional position is reported as a Tier 1 demonstration. Do not cite as technical evidence. Useful for discourse analysis and for identifying where overclaiming occurs. If a Tier 6 source makes a specific claim, trace it to its primary source before using it.

How to Read This Tool

Apply the tier framework to assess the evidential weight of any SBSP claim. For each claim encountered, identify the source tier of the document it originates in, then assess the claim's own evidential basis within that document. A Tier 2 feasibility study (document tier) may contain Tier 1 experimental results, Tier 2 modelling conclusions, and Tier 5 institutional framing in the same document — each claim must be assessed at its own evidential level. The dossier should be used in conjunction with the Claim Boundary Classifier tool for comprehensive claim assessment.

Methodology Boundary

Source tier classifications reflect this site's evidence governance methodology as documented in /methodology/ and /sources/. Tier assignments represent editorial classification based on publication type, peer review status, institutional independence, and methodological transparency. The dossier does not evaluate the internal quality of individual documents — it classifies their evidence tier based on structural characteristics. Documents within the same tier vary in quality.

Claim Boundary

This dossier classifies evidence source types for SBSP. It does not claim that higher-tier sources are correct, that lower-tier sources are without value, or that any source definitively establishes or refutes SBSP viability. Evidence tier classification provides a framework for appropriate epistemic weighting, not a binary include/exclude decision.
