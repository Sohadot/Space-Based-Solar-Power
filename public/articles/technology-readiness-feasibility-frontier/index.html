Technology Readiness and the SBSP Feasibility Frontier

Technology and Feasibility

Technology readiness level is a standard framework for assessing how close a technology is to operational deployment. Applied to space-based solar power, TRL analysis reveals that the key SBSP subsystems are at very different readiness stages — some components are mature, some have been demonstrated in space, and some remain at laboratory or conceptual stage. This brief maps the TRL landscape across SBSP's critical subsystems and explains what the uneven readiness profile means for the path to system integration.

Strategic Thesis

SBSP is not a single technology with a single TRL — it is a system of interdependent subsystems, each with its own readiness level. System integration at SBSP scale cannot begin until all critical subsystems reach sufficient individual readiness. The bottleneck subsystems — particularly in-space assembly at scale and integrated power transmission chain performance — currently define the outer boundary of SBSP's feasibility frontier.

Institutional Context

ESA SOLARIS, JAXA's SSPS programme, and the Caltech SSPP project have each contributed to advancing TRL in specific subsystems. The Caltech MAPLE experiment (2023) advanced the TRL of integrated phased-array WPT in space. JAXA and Mitsubishi Electric have conducted ground-based demonstrations of high-efficiency WPT components. Northrop Grumman's SSPIDR programme targets the phased array transmitter subsystem specifically. No programme has yet demonstrated full end-to-end system integration at any relevant scale.

Technical Context

At the component level, high-efficiency multi-junction solar cells for space are at TRL 9 for conventional satellite applications. Phased array antennas for communication are mature. The SBSP-specific combination — integrated solar-transmitter tile capable of collecting and beaming simultaneously, at the required efficiency and scale — is at significantly lower readiness. In-space assembly of large structures has been demonstrated for the International Space Station, but the scale and mass of SBSP structures would require capabilities not yet validated. Robotic on-orbit servicing is in early development.

Feasibility Boundary

No single TRL number applies to SBSP as a system. Individual component TRL figures should not be used to imply system-level readiness. The feasibility frontier is defined by the lowest-readiness critical subsystem, which for current SBSP programmes is typically in-space assembly at scale and end-to-end transmission chain performance.

Claim Boundary

This brief maps TRL status across SBSP subsystems based on published programme information and peer-reviewed research. It does not claim that any subsystem is deployment-ready or that current TRL progress implies a specific deployment timeline. TRL progress in one subsystem does not imply equivalent progress across the system.
