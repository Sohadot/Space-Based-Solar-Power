SBSP and Defense Energy Logistics: The Military Rationale for In-Orbit Power

Energy Sovereignty and AI Demand

Military and defence organisations have a distinct strategic rationale for studying space-based solar power that differs from the civilian energy transition case. Forward-deployed military operations face severe energy logistics constraints — fuel supply chains are vulnerable, expensive, and operationally limiting. SBSP, in the defence framing, is not primarily an electricity supply option for national grids — it is a potential power delivery system that could eliminate the logistics burden of fuel resupply in remote or contested environments.

Strategic Thesis

The defence energy logistics case for SBSP rests on the strategic value of eliminating liquid fuel supply chains to forward-operating bases or remote installations. In the defence framing, the cost economics of SBSP can look very different from the civilian electricity market case — because the comparison is not against grid electricity prices but against the full delivered cost of fuel in austere or contested environments, which can be orders of magnitude higher.

Institutional Context

US defence investment in SBSP-related technology has been sustained and structured. The Air Force Research Laboratory (AFRL) has a long-standing SBSP research programme. Northrop Grumman's SSPIDR programme, funded by AFRL, is specifically designed to advance the phased array transmitter technology needed for beamed power delivery. The Naval Research Laboratory (NRL) has conducted WPT research with defence logistics applications. The DoD's broader interest in energy resilience and forward power has provided sustained institutional support for this research direction.

Technical Context

Defence applications of SBSP or SBSP-derived WPT technology span a range of scales — from beaming power to small forward-operating bases to providing significant power to larger installations. WPT in a defence context does not require the full orbital system at civilian grid scale — it could in principle be developed as a niche capability serving high-value, power-constrained military environments before it reaches commercial grid scale. This defence pathway may run in parallel with, and potentially ahead of, civilian SBSP development.

Feasibility Boundary

No defence SBSP system has been deployed. SSPIDR is a technology demonstration programme validating components, not a deployed system. The defence logistics case depends on WPT technology reaching performance levels that have not yet been demonstrated at operationally relevant ranges and power levels.

Claim Boundary

This brief documents the defence energy logistics rationale for SBSP investment and the institutional research activities that reflect it. It does not claim that any defence SBSP system is operational, that SSPIDR has demonstrated operational capability, or that the defence case confirms civilian deployment viability. Defence applications context and civilian infrastructure context represent distinct deployment scenarios with different requirements.
