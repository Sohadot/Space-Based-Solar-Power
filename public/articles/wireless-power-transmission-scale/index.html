Wireless Power Transmission at Scale: Technical Thresholds and Current Progress

Technology and Feasibility

Wireless power transmission is the defining technical capability of space-based solar power. It is also one of the most actively researched and most frequently misrepresented aspects of the technology. This brief examines what WPT at SBSP-relevant scale requires technically, what current demonstrations have established, what efficiency thresholds remain unresolved at system scale, and why the gap between component-level results and end-to-end transmission chain performance is significant.

Strategic Thesis

WPT demonstrations including the Caltech MAPLE experiment and JAXA ground tests have confirmed that microwave power transmission is physically achievable in space-relevant conditions. They have not confirmed that end-to-end transmission efficiency, beam control at operational ranges, and power handling at gigawatt scale are simultaneously achievable. The distinction between component-level demonstration and system-level validation is analytically critical.

Institutional Context

Multiple programmes are actively developing WPT technology for SBSP. Caltech SSPP demonstrated targeted wireless power transfer in space for the first time in 2023. Northrop Grumman's SSPIDR programme is advancing phased array transmitter technology for the US Air Force Research Laboratory. Mitsubishi Electric has conducted ground-based WPT demonstrations in Japan in support of JAXA's SSPS programme. These activities represent genuine technology development, not conceptual work.

Technical Context

SBSP microwave WPT requires a phased array transmitter capable of forming and steering a precise beam over tens of thousands of kilometres. At the transmitter, power handling requirements are in the range of hundreds of megawatts to gigawatts for an operational system. Phased array beamforming at this scale requires coherent phase control across thousands of transmitter elements. Receiving efficiency at the rectenna depends on beam uniformity, frequency, and rectifying element performance. The full transmission chain (DC to microwave, propagation, microwave to DC) has cumulative efficiency losses at each stage. Current demonstrations operate at scales orders of magnitude below operational requirements.

Feasibility Boundary

No end-to-end WPT system at SBSP-relevant power levels or transmission distances has been demonstrated. Component-level results are promising but cannot be directly extrapolated to system-level performance without intermediate-scale demonstrations. Regulatory status for operational microwave transmission from space to ground is unresolved. Efficiency figures from laboratory and small-scale demonstrations do not reflect system-integrated performance.

Claim Boundary

This brief documents the technical state of WPT research relevant to SBSP. It does not claim that WPT at operational scale has been demonstrated, that current efficiency results are system-representative, or that regulatory approval for space-to-ground microwave transmission is assured. All performance data cited is from the referenced study or experiment context.
