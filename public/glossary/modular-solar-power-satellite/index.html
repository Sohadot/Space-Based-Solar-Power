Modular Solar Power Satellite

Glossary Term

An SBSP platform built from multiple individually launched components assembled incrementally in orbit.

A modular solar power satellite is an SBSP system architecture in which the overall platform is composed of discrete units that can be launched separately and assembled or aggregated in orbit to build the final system incrementally. Modularity is a proposed solution to the central SBSP constraint of mass-to-orbit economics: instead of launching a single enormous platform, many smaller units are launched over time on smaller or more cost-effective rockets, and the orbital assembly challenge replaces the single-launch mass challenge. Modular approaches introduce their own technical challenges including on-orbit docking and assembly, structural integrity of assembled modules, power routing between modules, and maintaining beam coherence across a distributed structure. Modular designs are explored by NASA, ESA, JAXA, and academic programs as a potential pathway to incremental SBSP development.

Claim Boundary

Modular SBSP architectures are studied in research and concept phases. No modular solar power satellite has been assembled or demonstrated in orbit.
