Laser Transmission Pathway

Glossary Term

The end-to-end laser energy pathway from orbital platform through atmosphere to terrestrial photovoltaic receiver.

The laser transmission pathway in SBSP encompasses the complete chain from conversion of solar or DC power to laser radiation, through beam formation and optical steering, across the atmospheric propagation path, to a photovoltaic or thermophotovoltaic receiver where laser energy is converted back to electricity. Laser-based SBSP concepts offer smaller transmitter and receiver apertures compared to microwave approaches due to shorter wavelengths, but atmospheric effects including absorption, scattering, and turbulence create different and often more severe efficiency losses, particularly under adverse weather. Safety requirements for laser beams at the power intensities required for SBSP are stringent. Laser pathways are studied for specific applications including remote power for space missions and niche terrestrial scenarios where receiver aperture constraints favor optical approaches.

Claim Boundary

Laser power transmission is demonstrated at laboratory scale and in directed-energy research. Orbital-to-ground laser SBSP has not been deployed.
