Disaster Response Power

Glossary Term

Emergency electrical power provision in response to natural disasters, infrastructure failures, or humanitarian crises.

Disaster response power refers to the emergency provision of electrical energy during and after natural disasters, infrastructure attacks, or humanitarian crises when normal grid infrastructure has been damaged or destroyed. Disaster response power is typically provided through portable generators, microgrids, or military mobile power systems. SBSP has been discussed as a potential disaster response tool because an orbital system could potentially direct a power beam to a portable receiving station deployed to an affected area, providing power without depending on ground-based fuel supply chains or damaged grid infrastructure. This application faces practical challenges including the deployment time of receiving equipment, orbital coverage availability, and the cost and complexity of the orbital system relative to conventional alternatives.

Claim Boundary

Disaster response power needs are documented. SBSP for disaster response has been studied conceptually but not operationally demonstrated.
