Remote Power Supply

Glossary Term

Provision of electrical power to locations distant from major grid infrastructure or conventional fuel supply chains.

Remote power supply refers to the challenge of delivering reliable electrical energy to locations that are not economically or practically connected to large centralized grid infrastructure, including remote communities, military forward operating bases, disaster zones, island systems, and polar installations. Remote power is typically expensive because conventional fuel supply requires long transport distances, and grid extension to remote areas carries high capital costs. SBSP is studied as a potential remote power solution because an orbital transmitter can, in principle, direct power to a portable or modular rectenna anywhere within its coverage area without requiring terrestrial grid infrastructure. The receiving infrastructure must still be deployed and maintained, and SBSP must be economically competitive with alternative remote power solutions.

Claim Boundary

Remote power challenges are well-documented. SBSP as a remote power solution has not been commercially demonstrated.
