Beam Steering

Glossary Term

Active redirection of an energy beam to track terrestrial receivers or shift power delivery between sites.

Beam steering is the capability to redirect an energy beam from one target receiver to another, or to track a moving receiver, using electronic or mechanical control. In SBSP systems, beam steering enables an orbital platform to track a terrestrial receiver as the satellite moves through its orbit, or to shift power delivery between receiving sites depending on demand. Electronic beam steering through phased arrays is preferred over mechanical approaches because it allows rapid redirection without moving parts at orbital altitudes. Beam steering range and speed determine the operational flexibility of an SBSP system, including the ability to respond to demand shifts, receiver outages, or safety conditions requiring immediate beam redirection.

Claim Boundary

Electronic beam steering is a mature technology in phased-array applications. Orbital-scale beam steering for SBSP power delivery has not been demonstrated in operational systems.
