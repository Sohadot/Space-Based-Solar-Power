Technology Readiness

Glossary Term

A general assessment of how mature a technology is relative to the requirements of its intended operational application.

Technology readiness is a general assessment of how advanced a technology is toward meeting the requirements of its intended operational application. In SBSP contexts, technology readiness is often discussed using the Technology Readiness Level (TRL) scale, which ranges from TRL 1 (basic principles observed) to TRL 9 (system proven in operational environment). Different components of the SBSP system chain — photovoltaics, phased array transmitters, structural assembly in orbit, rectenna arrays, power conditioning — operate at different TRL levels, and the system-level readiness is typically limited by the least mature component. Technology readiness assessments must be interpreted carefully: high TRL for a component in terrestrial or laboratory context does not automatically translate to readiness for the scale, environmental conditions, and system integration required by SBSP.

Claim Boundary

The TRL framework is an established assessment tool. Published TRL values for SBSP components vary by source and must be evaluated against specific system requirements they describe.
