Strategic Energy Logistics

Glossary Term

Planning and provision of energy supply for strategic operations, national security, or long-range missions.

Strategic energy logistics refers to the planning, supply chain management, and infrastructure development needed to ensure energy availability for national security operations, military expeditionary activities, critical infrastructure, and long-duration strategic missions where conventional fuel supply chains may be unavailable or unreliable. Energy logistics is a recognized vulnerability in modern military and national security planning: fuel supply lines are targets, remote operations are logistically constrained, and dependence on geopolitically volatile fuel sources creates strategic risk. SBSP is discussed in strategic energy logistics contexts because an orbital energy system could theoretically deliver power to virtually any point on Earth or in cislunar space with minimal dependence on terrestrial supply chains.

Claim Boundary

Strategic energy logistics challenges are documented in defense and policy literature. SBSP as a strategic logistics solution is a conceptual discussion without operational demonstration.
