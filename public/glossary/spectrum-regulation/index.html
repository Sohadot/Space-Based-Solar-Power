Spectrum Regulation

Glossary Term

The international governance framework allocating rights to use specific electromagnetic frequency bands.

Spectrum regulation refers to the national and international framework for assigning rights to use specific electromagnetic frequency bands, administered globally through the International Telecommunication Union (ITU) and nationally through regulatory agencies such as the FCC in the United States and Ofcom in the United Kingdom. SBSP systems using microwave power beaming would require allocation of specific frequency bands for high-power point-to-point transmission not covered under standard telecommunications frameworks. Obtaining spectrum allocations for commercial SBSP would require internationally coordinated regulatory processes, potential frequency coordination with satellite communications and radio astronomy users, and new regulatory frameworks that do not yet exist for high-power orbital-to-ground energy transmission.

Claim Boundary

Spectrum regulation frameworks are well-established. SBSP-specific spectrum allocations do not currently exist and would require new international regulatory processes.
