Island Grid

Glossary Term

An electrical grid operating in isolation from larger interconnected networks, typically in geographically isolated regions.

An island grid is an electrical power system that operates independently of major interconnected grid networks, typically found on physically isolated islands, remote communities, or other locations where interconnection with broader grid infrastructure is physically or economically impractical. Island grids face challenges including limited generation diversity, vulnerability to single-point failures, high fuel import costs, and difficulty integrating high proportions of variable renewable generation without substantial storage. SBSP has been discussed as a potential solution for island grids because it could provide continuous baseload-compatible power to a receiving installation without requiring interconnection with mainland grids. Pacific island nations, remote Arctic communities, and military island installations are among the scenarios commonly discussed.

Claim Boundary

Island grid challenges are well-documented. SBSP as an island grid power solution has not been commercially demonstrated.
