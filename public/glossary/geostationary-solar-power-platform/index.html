Geostationary Solar Power Platform

Glossary Term

An SBSP system positioned in geostationary orbit to provide continuous power to a fixed terrestrial receiver.

A geostationary solar power platform is a solar power satellite or SBSP system deployed in geostationary orbit at approximately 35,786 km altitude, where it remains stationary relative to a fixed point on Earth's equator. The geostationary position is favored in many SBSP concepts because it enables continuous power delivery to a dedicated rectenna site without complex beam tracking between a moving transmitter and a fixed receiver. However, geostationary orbit also imposes significant constraints: the transmission distance is large, increasing beam divergence and transmission loss; the altitude requires enormous photovoltaic area to produce useful power at the receiver; and launch costs to geostationary transfer orbit are significantly higher than to lower orbits.

Claim Boundary

Geostationary orbit is well-understood as an operational environment. Solar power satellites in geostationary orbit have been studied extensively but not deployed.
