Power Beaming

Glossary Term

Wireless transmission of electrical energy using directed electromagnetic radiation, typically microwave or laser.

Power beaming is the wireless transmission of electrical energy from a source to a receiver using directed electromagnetic radiation, typically at microwave or laser frequencies. In the SBSP context, power beaming is the core transmission mechanism that enables energy collected in orbit to reach terrestrial users without physical connection. The two primary approaches — microwave power beaming and laser power beaming — differ in atmospheric interaction, efficiency, safety profile, regulatory environment, and receiver design requirements. Power beaming has been demonstrated at laboratory and small-scale field levels. Extending these demonstrations to the power densities, transmission distances, and system sizes required for commercial SBSP represents a major unsolved engineering and economic challenge.

Claim Boundary

Power beaming has been demonstrated at small scale. Commercial SBSP power beaming at grid-relevant power levels and orbital distances has not been achieved.
