Solar Power Satellite Array

Glossary Term

Multiple coordinated solar power satellites operating as a collective SBSP collection and transmission system.

A solar power satellite array is a configuration in which multiple discrete solar power satellites are positioned and coordinated to function as a collective energy collection and transmission system. Arrays may be designed to concentrate beam energy toward a single receiver, provide redundancy against individual satellite failure, or cover different reception points depending on demand patterns. Array configurations are studied in the context of scalable SBSP architectures where a single large monolithic satellite is considered impractical for initial deployment, and phased approaches to power collection and beam formation offer alternative technical pathways. Array coordination introduces additional challenges of beam synchronization, interference management, and orbital positioning.

Claim Boundary

Array architectures for SBSP are studied in concept and system design phases. No multi-satellite SBSP array has been deployed.
