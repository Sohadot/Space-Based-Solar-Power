Orbital Energy Reference Layer

Glossary Term

A conceptual framework treating Earth orbit as an energy infrastructure layer for solar collection and terrestrial power delivery.

The orbital energy reference layer is a conceptual organizing framework that treats Earth orbit as a potential infrastructure layer for energy collection and distribution, analogous to how terrestrial infrastructure layers support energy transmission and distribution on the ground. In this framework, solar power satellites, power beaming systems, receiving infrastructure, and grid interconnection represent different layers of a complete orbital energy infrastructure stack. The term is used in strategic, policy, and reference-architecture contexts rather than engineering specifications. It helps frame SBSP not as a single device or program but as a category of infrastructure development with implications for energy sovereignty, national strategy, space logistics, and long-term industrialization of the space environment.

Claim Boundary

The orbital energy reference layer is a conceptual framing used in strategic and reference documents. It does not describe an existing deployed infrastructure layer.
