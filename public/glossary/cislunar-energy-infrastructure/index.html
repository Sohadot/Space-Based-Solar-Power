Cislunar Energy Infrastructure

Glossary Term

Energy systems intended to serve the operational zone between Earth and the Moon.

Cislunar energy infrastructure refers to power generation, storage, and distribution systems designed to serve the cislunar operational space — the region between Earth's atmosphere and the Moon's orbit, including Earth-Moon Lagrange points, lunar orbit, and lunar transit trajectories. As space agencies and commercial operators develop lunar programs, orbital stations, and transportation systems operating in the cislunar environment, a coherent energy supply architecture becomes a foundational requirement. SBSP concepts and orbital power relay architectures are discussed as components of eventual cislunar energy infrastructure. The cislunar domain is increasingly recognized as strategic space with implications for national security, commercial resource exploitation, and long-term human expansion.

Claim Boundary

Cislunar operational concepts are under active development by space agencies. Cislunar energy infrastructure at operational scale has not been deployed.
