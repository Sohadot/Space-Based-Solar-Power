Defense Energy Resilience

Glossary Term

The capacity of military and defense infrastructure to maintain power supply under adversarial conditions or supply disruptions.

Defense energy resilience is the capability of military and defense systems to maintain reliable access to electrical power during adversarial attacks, grid disruptions, natural disasters, expeditionary operations, or any scenario that compromises conventional energy supply infrastructure. Defense energy resilience is a priority concern for modern military forces because contemporary warfare depends heavily on electronic systems, communications, logistics, and intelligence infrastructure that require continuous power. SBSP is discussed as a potential contribution to defense energy resilience because an orbital power system would be physically independent of terrestrial grid infrastructure and supply chains that can be targeted or disrupted. This potential must be weighed against the vulnerability of orbital assets to anti-satellite capabilities.

Claim Boundary

Defense energy resilience requirements are documented. SBSP's contribution to defense energy resilience is a strategic concept that has not been operationally tested.
