Space-to-Earth Power Transmission

Glossary Term

The complete pathway moving electrical energy from an orbital platform to Earth-based receivers.

Space-to-earth power transmission describes the complete energy delivery pathway from an orbital platform to a terrestrial receiving station. It encompasses beam generation, power conversion aboard the orbital platform, beam propagation through space and the atmosphere, reception at a terrestrial rectenna or photovoltaic receiver, and conversion to grid-compatible electricity. The term is directional and system-level: it defines the full chain between generation source and end use, including the transmission medium choice between microwave and laser frequencies, the atmospheric interaction profile, and the energy conversion efficiency at each step. Challenges include atmospheric absorption, beam divergence over long distances, beam control accuracy, receiver size, and safety governance.

Claim Boundary

The concept and component technologies are established in research literature. Commercial space-to-earth power transmission at grid scale has not been demonstrated.
