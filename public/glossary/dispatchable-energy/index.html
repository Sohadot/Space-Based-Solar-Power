Dispatchable Energy

Glossary Term

Energy that can be generated and delivered to the grid on demand at controlled rates and timing.

Dispatchable energy refers to electricity generation capacity that can be activated, adjusted, or shut down according to grid demand signals, as opposed to variable renewable generation that depends on natural resource availability. Grid operators rely on dispatchable sources to balance supply and demand in real time and to respond to unexpected demand peaks or supply failures. SBSP concepts are sometimes described as potentially dispatchable because the orbital transmitter can in principle direct its beam, adjust power level, or switch between receivers, giving operators some control over when and where power is delivered. However, this potential dispatchability is subject to orbital geometry constraints, beam safety requirements, transmission system availability, and the as-yet-undemonstrated control systems required for commercial operation.

Claim Boundary

Dispatchable energy as a concept is established in power systems. SBSP dispatchability is a design claim that has not been validated in operational systems.
