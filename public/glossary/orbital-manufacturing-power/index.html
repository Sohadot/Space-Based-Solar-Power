Orbital Manufacturing Power

Glossary Term

Electrical power required for manufacturing activities conducted in Earth orbit.

Orbital manufacturing power refers to the electrical energy needed to support material processing, fabrication, and assembly activities performed in Earth orbit. As the space industry explores orbital manufacturing for products that benefit from microgravity, vacuum, or continuous solar exposure — including ultra-pure materials, fiber optics, pharmaceutical crystals, and in-space structural components — the power required for these processes becomes a design and economic constraint. An orbital solar power system could provide the large-scale continuous power needed for industrial manufacturing processes in orbit without depending on fuel supplies from Earth. The connection between SBSP technology development and orbital manufacturing creates a potential economic use case that complements the terrestrial power delivery mission.

Claim Boundary

Orbital manufacturing concepts are under early commercial development. Orbital manufacturing at industrial scale requiring orbital power infrastructure has not been demonstrated.
