Beam Control

Glossary Term

The processes maintaining accurate direction and intensity of a transmitted energy beam toward its target receiver.

Beam control in power beaming systems refers to the engineering processes and hardware systems responsible for pointing, tracking, and maintaining an energy beam directed at a specified receiver location while managing beam quality, divergence, and unintended radiation. In the SBSP context, beam control must maintain alignment between an orbital transmitter moving at orbital velocity and a terrestrial rectenna or photovoltaic receiver, correcting for orbital motion, atmospheric variations, transmitter platform dynamics, and potential interference. Beam control is implemented through retrodirective steering arrays, pilot signal feedback from the receiver, or combined approaches. Failures or degradation in beam control create both efficiency losses and safety hazards requiring automatic beam shutdown systems.

Claim Boundary

Beam control techniques are established in phased-array radar and directed-energy research. Precision beam control for commercial SBSP power delivery has not been demonstrated at operational scale.
