Space-Based Power Station

Glossary Term

An orbital facility designed for large-scale solar energy collection and wireless transmission to terrestrial users.

A space-based power station is an orbital facility whose primary function is large-scale electrical energy collection and wireless transmission to one or more terrestrial receiving sites. The term is used interchangeably with solar power satellite in many contexts, though space-based power station can also encompass concepts that use energy sources beyond direct solar photovoltaics. In all current SBSP concepts, solar irradiance is the energy source. A space-based power station would operate at a scale fundamentally different from space power systems supplying only satellites or space stations: it would be designed to generate power equivalent to a conventional terrestrial power plant, typically cited as hundreds of megawatts to gigawatts, and deliver it to a terrestrial grid.

Claim Boundary

The concept of a space-based power station is established in SBSP literature. No such facility has been constructed or operated.
