Orbital Power Generation

Glossary Term

Electrical power generation performed by systems operating in Earth orbit and intended for export to terrestrial or in-space users.

Orbital power generation refers to the production of electrical energy by systems operating in Earth orbit, primarily through solar photovoltaic conversion of solar radiation above the atmosphere. In the SBSP context, the term emphasizes the orbital operating environment: solar irradiance is approximately 1,361 W/m² and is available without weather or nighttime interruption for much of a geostationary orbit. Orbital power generation does not automatically produce net benefit; the generated energy must be converted, transmitted, received, and reconverted to electricity on Earth with acceptable total system efficiency. The concept is distinguished from space power systems that supply only local spacecraft demand by its intent to export power to terrestrial users or future in-space facilities.

Claim Boundary

Solar power generation in orbit is well-established at spacecraft scale. Generation at grid-relevant scales for SBSP has not been deployed.
