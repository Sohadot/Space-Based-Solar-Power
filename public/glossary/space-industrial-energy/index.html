Space Industrial Energy

Glossary Term

Energy supply for manufacturing, processing, and industrial operations conducted in space environments.

Space industrial energy refers to the electrical and thermal power required to support manufacturing, resource processing, and production activities conducted in space environments, including Earth orbit, lunar orbit, the lunar surface, and potentially other destinations. As concepts for space-based manufacturing, asteroid mining, in-space propellant production, and orbital construction mature, the power requirements for these activities become a design constraint. SBSP technology is relevant to space industrial energy because an orbital power generation system could supply energy not only downward to terrestrial users but also to in-space industrial facilities, spacecraft, and propellant depots operating in the same orbital or cislunar environment. This dual-use potential connects SBSP to the broader space economy development agenda.

Claim Boundary

Space industrial activity is at early commercial and concept stages. Space industrial energy requirements from SBSP sources have not been operationally demonstrated.
