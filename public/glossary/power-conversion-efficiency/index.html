Power Conversion Efficiency

Glossary Term

The ratio of usable electrical power output to energy input at any conversion stage in the SBSP energy chain.

Power conversion efficiency in the SBSP context refers to the ratio of useful electrical power output to the energy input at any individual stage of the SBSP energy chain, or to the overall system efficiency from solar irradiance to grid-delivered electricity. Each stage of the SBSP chain — photovoltaic conversion in orbit, conversion to microwave or laser, atmospheric transmission, reception and rectification, and power conditioning — has its own efficiency factor, and these multiply together to determine the overall system conversion efficiency. High efficiency at each stage is critical because SBSP system economics depend strongly on the power delivered per unit of orbital platform mass or per unit of cost. Published efficiency values for individual components may not apply directly to full-scale SBSP systems operating in the combined conditions of space and atmospheric transmission.

Claim Boundary

Component-level efficiency values are published in engineering literature. Integrated SBSP system efficiency at commercial scale has not been demonstrated.
