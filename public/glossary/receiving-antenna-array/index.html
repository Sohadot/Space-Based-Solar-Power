Receiving Antenna Array

Glossary Term

An array of antenna elements at the terrestrial receiving station designed to capture transmitted power beam energy.

A receiving antenna array is the organized collection of individual antenna elements at the terrestrial end of a power beaming system, arranged to capture energy from an incoming electromagnetic beam. In microwave SBSP systems, the receiving antenna array is the core component of the rectenna field. Array design must balance individual element spacing for efficient energy capture, structural requirements for large-area deployment, electrical interconnection for power aggregation, and accommodation of varying beam intensity profiles across the array aperture. The aperture size of the receiving antenna array — typically measured in km² for SBSP — is determined by the transmitted beam power density at the receiver and acceptable power density limits for safety and land use regulations.

Claim Boundary

Receiving antenna arrays are demonstrated at research scale in wireless power transmission experiments. Commercial SBSP-scale receiving arrays have not been deployed.
