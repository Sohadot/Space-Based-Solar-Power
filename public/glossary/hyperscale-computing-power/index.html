Hyperscale Computing Power

Glossary Term

Electrical power required by very large data center and AI infrastructure at scales that strain conventional energy systems.

Hyperscale computing power refers to the electrical energy requirements of very large data center facilities and artificial intelligence computing infrastructure, where individual facilities may consume hundreds of megawatts to gigawatts continuously. The rapid growth of AI training, inference, and cloud computing workloads has driven dramatic increases in data center energy demand, creating pressure on electricity grids, increasing carbon footprint concerns, and accelerating interest in new power sources. SBSP is discussed in this context as a potential future energy source for hyperscale facilities because it could provide continuous, location-flexible, and potentially low-carbon power at the scale required. This connection between SBSP and AI energy demand makes the field relevant to technology and strategic planning discussions beyond traditional energy policy.

Claim Boundary

Hyperscale computing power demand trends are documented. SBSP as a solution for hyperscale power demand has not been commercially demonstrated.
