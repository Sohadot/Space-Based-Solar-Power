Long Duration Space Power

Glossary Term

Power systems designed for continuous reliable operation over extended periods in the space environment.

Long duration space power refers to power generation and supply systems that must operate continuously and reliably over months, years, or decades in the space environment without replacement or significant maintenance from Earth. Space-based solar power satellites, lunar surface power systems, deep space probes, and long-duration crewed missions all require power systems that can function through radiation exposure, temperature cycling, micrometeorite impacts, and component aging with high reliability. The development of durable power systems for long-duration space operation is both a prerequisite for SBSP and a technology area that SBSP development would advance. Degradation rates of photovoltaic cells in the space radiation environment, longevity of power electronics, and maintenance access in orbit are key challenges.

Claim Boundary

Long-duration space power is a documented engineering challenge. SBSP-scale long-duration space power operation has not been demonstrated at commercial scale.
