Space Solar Power System

Glossary Term

The complete integrated architecture for collecting solar energy in space, transmitting it to Earth, and delivering it to users.

A space solar power system (SSPS) is the complete integrated architecture encompassing orbital solar collection, power conversion, wireless power transmission, atmospheric transit, and ground-based reception and grid delivery. Unlike treating SBSP as only a satellite concept, the SSPS framing emphasizes that every component of the chain must function at scale for the system to work. The term is used particularly in Japanese national and JAXA program documentation, though similar system-level framing appears in NASA and ESA studies. The SSPS concept requires concurrent advances in orbital photovoltaic efficiency, power beaming performance, atmospheric propagation, rectenna scale, and terrestrial grid integration before commercial operation becomes credible.

Claim Boundary

System-level SSPS concepts are well-documented in national program literature. No complete SSPS has been deployed at commercial scale as of 2026.
