Space Energy Grid

Glossary Term

A coordinated architecture of multiple orbital energy assets providing distributed and redundant power supply.

A space energy grid is a long-horizon concept in which multiple orbital energy collection and transmission platforms operate in a coordinated architecture to provide continuous, distributed, and strategically diversified power supply. Unlike a single solar power satellite, a space energy grid would use multiple assets in complementary orbits or positions to provide redundancy, coverage across multiple receiver locations, and the ability to redirect power toward different demand centers. The concept extends SBSP from a single point-to-point power system to an orbital energy infrastructure layer analogous to terrestrial electrical grids. No space energy grid has been deployed; the concept remains in the strategic planning and concept phase.

Claim Boundary

The space energy grid concept is described in strategic and academic SBSP literature. No space energy grid exists or has been formally approved for development.
