Critical Infrastructure Power

Glossary Term

Power provision essential to systems whose failure would cause severe disruption to public safety, security, or national function.

Critical infrastructure power refers to the electrical supply requirements of systems and facilities whose continuous operation is essential to public safety, national security, economic function, or population welfare, including data centers, hospitals, communications networks, water treatment systems, defense installations, and financial infrastructure. Resilience of power supply to critical infrastructure is a priority concern in national security and emergency management planning. SBSP is discussed in this context as a potential alternative or supplementary power source for critical infrastructure that could provide power resilience against terrestrial disruptions including natural disasters, grid failures, or physical attacks. The relevance of SBSP to critical infrastructure power depends on reliability, cost, and availability of orbital systems that have not been built.

Claim Boundary

Critical infrastructure power requirements are well-documented. SBSP's role in critical infrastructure power supply has not been demonstrated operationally.
