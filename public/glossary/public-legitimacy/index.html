Public Legitimacy

Glossary Term

The social and political acceptance that SBSP projects must earn from communities, regulators, and international bodies.

Public legitimacy in the SBSP context refers to the social and political acceptance that SBSP technology and infrastructure projects would need from affected populations, policymakers, and international communities to move from concept to deployment. Public legitimacy is distinct from technical feasibility or economic viability: a technology can be technically proven and economically competitive yet fail to achieve deployment because it lacks public trust or political support. SBSP faces potential public legitimacy challenges related to perceived risks of high-power beam transmission, land requirements for rectenna fields, spectrum interference concerns, and broader anxieties about militarization of space or unequal access to orbital energy resources. Public legitimacy must be earned through transparent communication, rigorous safety governance, and sustained community engagement.

Claim Boundary

The importance of public acceptance for infrastructure projects is well-documented. SBSP public legitimacy challenges are speculative since no public SBSP deployment has been proposed at the scale requiring formal community approval.
