Energy Security

Glossary Term

A nation's or organization's ability to maintain reliable access to sufficient energy without unacceptable supply vulnerability.

Energy security refers to the conditions under which a nation, organization, or critical system can reliably access sufficient energy to meet operational, economic, and strategic needs without unacceptable vulnerability to supply disruption, price volatility, or geopolitical pressure. Energy security encompasses source diversity, supply chain resilience, storage capacity, transmission infrastructure redundancy, and the absence of undue dependence on hostile or unreliable suppliers. SBSP is discussed in energy security contexts as a potential pathway to space-derived energy independent of terrestrial geopolitical supply chains, though this strategic benefit must be weighed against the new vulnerabilities introduced by orbital asset dependence, space situational awareness requirements, and the geopolitical complexity of orbital infrastructure ownership and access.

Claim Boundary

Energy security as a concept is well-established in policy and strategic literature. SBSP's contribution to energy security has not been demonstrated in operational systems.
