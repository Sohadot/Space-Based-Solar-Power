Space Debris Risk

Glossary Term

The risk to SBSP platforms from orbital collision debris and the risk that SBSP operations contribute to debris accumulation.

Space debris risk in the SBSP context refers to both the risk that debris in Earth orbit poses to large SBSP platforms and the risk that SBSP development might contribute to debris accumulation through mission failures, structural degradation, or collisions. SBSP platforms would be among the largest human-made structures ever placed in orbit, making them significant targets for existing debris and potential contributors to the debris environment if they break up or shed components. The Kessler syndrome — a cascade of debris collisions rendering certain orbital regions unusable — is a long-term concern. SBSP concepts must address debris avoidance, impact resistance, end-of-life disposal, and the cumulative environmental impact of large-scale orbital industrial activity.

Claim Boundary

Space debris as an orbital safety concern is well-established. SBSP-specific debris risks depend on designs and orbital regimes that have not been finalized.
