Mission Critical Power

Glossary Term

Power provision essential to the success or safety of a specified operational mission.

Mission critical power refers to electrical energy provision where interruption or degradation would directly cause failure of a specified operational mission or create an unacceptable safety risk to personnel or systems. In defense and space contexts, mission critical power includes life support systems, command and control communications, navigation, propulsion, and intelligence collection. SBSP is discussed as a potential provider of mission critical power in scenarios where conventional terrestrial power infrastructure is unavailable, including expeditionary military operations, disaster relief, remote scientific missions, and eventually deep-space or lunar missions. The reliability standards for mission critical power systems are substantially higher than for commercial grid applications.

Claim Boundary

Mission critical power requirements are documented in defense and space system standards. SBSP as a mission critical power source has not been operationally demonstrated.
