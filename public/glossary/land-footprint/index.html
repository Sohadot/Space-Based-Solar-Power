Land Footprint

Glossary Term

The terrestrial area required for SBSP receiving infrastructure, including rectenna fields, buffer zones, and support facilities.

Land footprint in the SBSP context refers to the total terrestrial area that a receiving installation requires, including the active rectenna or photovoltaic receiver surface, safety buffer zones to maintain acceptable power density limits at the perimeter, access roads, support facilities, and grid connection infrastructure. For large-scale SBSP systems delivering hundreds of megawatts to gigawatts, rectenna field land footprints are typically estimated at tens to hundreds of square kilometers depending on allowed power density limits. Land footprint is a significant practical constraint because securing contiguous land areas of this size at reasonable cost, with appropriate grid access, community acceptance, and compatible land use, is challenging in densely populated or agriculturally productive regions.

Claim Boundary

Land footprint estimates for SBSP are derived from engineering analysis of power density requirements. Actual land acquisition for SBSP has not occurred.
