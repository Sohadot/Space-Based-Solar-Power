Phased Array Transmitter

Glossary Term

A multi-element antenna forming and steering a directed power beam through electronically controlled constructive interference.

A phased array transmitter is an antenna architecture in which many individual radiating elements emit electromagnetic signals with controlled phase relationships, creating constructive interference that forms and steers a directed beam without physical movement of the antenna structure. In SBSP, phased array transmitters are the primary proposed technology for generating and steering microwave power beams from orbital platforms toward terrestrial rectennas. The ability to electronically control beam direction, shape, and power density distribution enables precise targeting of terrestrial receivers and the retrodirective feedback control needed for beam safety. SBSP-scale phased array transmitters would require large aperture areas — potentially hundreds of meters to kilometers — to achieve useful power density at Earth's surface.

Claim Boundary

Phased array transmitters are mature technology in radar, satellite communications, and electronic warfare. SBSP-scale phased array transmitters for power delivery have not been deployed in orbit.
