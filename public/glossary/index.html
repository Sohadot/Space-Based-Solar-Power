SBSP Glossary

A governed glossary of space-based solar power, orbital energy infrastructure, wireless power transmission, rectennas, and related strategic terms.

Core Infrastructure

  • Space-Based Solar Power — Collecting solar energy in Earth orbit and transmitting it wirelessly to ground receivers for conversion to grid electricity.
  • Solar Power Satellite — A spacecraft in Earth orbit designed to collect solar energy and transmit it wirelessly to a ground receiver.
  • Geostationary Orbit — Earth orbit at 35,786 km altitude where satellites remain stationary above a fixed equatorial point, receiving near-continuous sunlight.
  • Space Solar Power System — The complete integrated architecture for collecting solar energy in space, transmitting it to Earth, and delivering it to users.
  • Space-to-Earth Power Transmission — The complete pathway moving electrical energy from an orbital platform to Earth-based receivers.
  • Orbital Power Generation — Electrical power generation performed by systems operating in Earth orbit and intended for export to terrestrial or in-space users.
  • Space Energy Grid — A coordinated architecture of multiple orbital energy assets providing distributed and redundant power supply.
  • Solar Power Satellite Array — Multiple coordinated solar power satellites operating as a collective SBSP collection and transmission system.
  • Modular Solar Power Satellite — An SBSP platform built from multiple individually launched components assembled incrementally in orbit.
  • Geostationary Solar Power Platform — An SBSP system positioned in geostationary orbit to provide continuous power to a fixed terrestrial receiver.
  • Space-Based Power Station — An orbital facility designed for large-scale solar energy collection and wireless transmission to terrestrial users.
  • Orbital Energy Reference Layer — A conceptual framework treating Earth orbit as an energy infrastructure layer for solar collection and terrestrial power delivery.

Transmission and Reception

  • Wireless Power Transmission — The transfer of electrical energy without physical conductors, using microwave or laser radiation as the transmission medium.
  • Microwave Power Beaming — Using microwave-frequency electromagnetic radiation to transmit energy wirelessly from an orbital source to a terrestrial rectenna array.
  • Laser Power Transmission — Using concentrated laser light to transmit energy wirelessly from an orbital source to a photovoltaic receiver.
  • Rectenna — A rectifying antenna array that converts received microwave energy into direct current electricity at the SBSP ground receiving station.
  • Power Beaming — Wireless transmission of electrical energy using directed electromagnetic radiation, typically microwave or laser.
  • Microwave Transmission Pathway — The end-to-end microwave energy pathway from orbital transmitter through atmosphere to terrestrial rectenna.
  • Laser Transmission Pathway — The end-to-end laser energy pathway from orbital platform through atmosphere to terrestrial photovoltaic receiver.
  • Beam Control — The processes maintaining accurate direction and intensity of a transmitted energy beam toward its target receiver.
  • Beam Steering — Active redirection of an energy beam to track terrestrial receivers or shift power delivery between sites.
  • Phased Array Transmitter — A multi-element antenna forming and steering a directed power beam through electronically controlled constructive interference.
  • Atmospheric Attenuation — Reduction of beam energy intensity as electromagnetic radiation passes through the atmosphere due to absorption and scattering.
  • Rectenna Field — A large-area deployment of rectenna elements forming an integrated ground-based microwave power receiving system.
  • Receiving Antenna Array — An array of antenna elements at the terrestrial receiving station designed to capture transmitted power beam energy.
  • Power Conversion Efficiency — The ratio of usable electrical power output to energy input at any conversion stage in the SBSP energy chain.

Grid and Energy Systems

  • Energy Sovereignty — A nation's capacity to control its own energy supply independently of external vulnerabilities, a core strategic argument for long-horizon SBSP development.
  • Baseload Power — Continuous stable electrical power generation meeting minimum grid demand at all times, regardless of weather or time of day.
  • Dispatchable Energy — Energy that can be generated and delivered to the grid on demand at controlled rates and timing.
  • Energy Security — A nation's or organization's ability to maintain reliable access to sufficient energy without unacceptable supply vulnerability.
  • Remote Power Supply — Provision of electrical power to locations distant from major grid infrastructure or conventional fuel supply chains.
  • Island Grid — An electrical grid operating in isolation from larger interconnected networks, typically in geographically isolated regions.
  • Critical Infrastructure Power — Power provision essential to systems whose failure would cause severe disruption to public safety, security, or national function.
  • Disaster Response Power — Emergency electrical power provision in response to natural disasters, infrastructure failures, or humanitarian crises.

Constraints and Governance

  • Launch Economics — The cost structure of delivering mass to orbit, the primary economic constraint determining whether SBSP can compete with terrestrial energy sources.
  • Technology Readiness Level — A NASA-originated scale from 1 to 9 measuring technology maturity, used to contextualize SBSP component and system readiness.
  • Mass to Orbit — The mass of material that must be transported from Earth to orbit to construct and operate an SBSP system.
  • Space Debris Risk — The risk to SBSP platforms from orbital collision debris and the risk that SBSP operations contribute to debris accumulation.
  • Spectrum Regulation — The international governance framework allocating rights to use specific electromagnetic frequency bands.
  • Public Legitimacy — The social and political acceptance that SBSP projects must earn from communities, regulators, and international bodies.
  • Safety Boundary — A defined limit on acceptable hazard exposure from SBSP operations, required for regulatory approval and safe operation.
  • Land Footprint — The terrestrial area required for SBSP receiving infrastructure, including rectenna fields, buffer zones, and support facilities.
  • Commercial Readiness — The technical, economic, and regulatory maturity required for SBSP to compete as a viable commercial energy product.
  • Technology Readiness — A general assessment of how mature a technology is relative to the requirements of its intended operational application.

Strategic Demand and Space Industry

  • Hyperscale Computing Power — Electrical power required by very large data center and AI infrastructure at scales that strain conventional energy systems.
  • Defense Energy Resilience — The capacity of military and defense infrastructure to maintain power supply under adversarial conditions or supply disruptions.
  • Lunar Power Infrastructure — Power systems designed to support sustained human and robotic operations on or near the Moon.
  • Space Industrial Energy — Energy supply for manufacturing, processing, and industrial operations conducted in space environments.
  • Orbital Manufacturing Power — Electrical power required for manufacturing activities conducted in Earth orbit.
  • Cislunar Energy Infrastructure — Energy systems intended to serve the operational zone between Earth and the Moon.
  • Strategic Energy Logistics — Planning and provision of energy supply for strategic operations, national security, or long-range missions.
  • Mission Critical Power — Power provision essential to the success or safety of a specified operational mission.
  • Long Duration Space Power — Power systems designed for continuous reliable operation over extended periods in the space environment.
