Safety Boundary

Glossary Term

A defined limit on acceptable hazard exposure from SBSP operations, required for regulatory approval and safe operation.

A safety boundary in the SBSP context is a defined threshold limiting potential hazard exposure to people, wildlife, aircraft, and other assets from the electromagnetic radiation produced by a power beaming system. Safety boundaries for microwave SBSP might include maximum permissible exposure levels for the beam footprint, exclusion zones around the rectenna field, beam intensity limits for transient illumination of off-target areas, and interlocking systems that terminate transmission if the beam deviates from the authorized target zone. Establishing appropriate safety boundaries requires rigorous dosimetry, risk modeling, regulatory review, and public consultation. Safety boundaries are a governance prerequisite for SBSP operation rather than a technical performance specification.

Claim Boundary

Safety standards for electromagnetic radiation exposure are established for many applications. SBSP-specific safety boundaries for large-scale power beaming do not yet exist as formal regulatory requirements.
