Rectenna Field

Glossary Term

A large-area deployment of rectenna elements forming an integrated ground-based microwave power receiving system.

A rectenna field is the complete terrestrial receiving installation for a microwave power beaming system, consisting of a large array of individual rectenna elements collectively covering sufficient area to receive, convert, and deliver transmitted microwave energy to the power grid. Individual rectenna elements are relatively simple devices combining an antenna and a rectifying diode circuit, but the field-level system involves interconnection, power conditioning, grid connection, structural support, land management, and safety infrastructure. SBSP concepts typically require rectenna fields covering hundreds of hectares to square kilometers to receive multi-gigawatt beams at acceptable power density. The land requirement and proximity to populated areas are significant planning and public-acceptance considerations.

Claim Boundary

Individual rectenna elements are well-demonstrated. Rectenna fields at the scale required for commercial SBSP have not been constructed.
