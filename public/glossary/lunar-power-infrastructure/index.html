Lunar Power Infrastructure

Glossary Term

Power systems designed to support sustained human and robotic operations on or near the Moon.

Lunar power infrastructure encompasses the electrical power generation, storage, transmission, and distribution systems required to support sustained lunar surface operations, lunar orbit stations, and transportation systems operating in the Earth-Moon system. As space agencies and commercial entities plan for permanent lunar presence through programs such as NASA's Artemis and ESA's lunar exploration initiatives, reliable power supply is a foundational requirement. SBSP technology and orbital power transmission concepts are relevant to lunar power infrastructure because some proposed lunar surface power solutions involve orbital relays, beamed power delivery to shadowed polar regions, or cislunar power architecture that draws on SBSP development heritage. Power for lunar manufacturing, life support, propellant production, and communication is among the key enabling constraints for sustained lunar operations.

Claim Boundary

Lunar power requirements are documented in space agency planning documents. Lunar power infrastructure has not been constructed at the scale required for sustained operations.
