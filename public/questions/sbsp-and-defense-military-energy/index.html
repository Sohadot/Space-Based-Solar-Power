Is space-based solar power relevant to defense and military energy?

Reference Question

Defense and military energy logistics have been a significant driver of interest in wireless power transmission and SBSP research. Military forward operating bases in remote or austere environments face the challenge of fuel supply: convoys transporting fuel are vulnerable, expensive, and logistically complex. The ability to deliver power wirelessly from orbit could theoretically eliminate fuel supply chains for forward power generation. The U.S. Naval Research Laboratory has conducted research into wireless power transmission for military applications. The U.S. Air Force Research Laboratory has studied related concepts. These programs represent genuine research interest, but they should not be interpreted as near-term operational capabilities. Wireless power delivery from orbit for military field use faces the same technology barriers as commercial SBSP: the transmitting and receiving infrastructure does not exist at operational scale. Defense organizations often maintain longer research time horizons than commercial investors, which partially explains why defense-adjacent institutions have sustained interest in WPT research.

Answer Boundary

Defense research programs are documented. Operational military SBSP does not exist. Research program existence should not be cited as near-term operational capability.
