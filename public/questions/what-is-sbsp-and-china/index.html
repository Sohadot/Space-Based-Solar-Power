What is the status of space-based solar power research in China?

Reference Question

China has conducted SBSP research for over a decade and has stated ambitions for long-term SBSP development. The China Academy of Space Technology (CAST) has published SBSP design concepts and roadmaps. Chinese researchers built a ground-based wireless power transmission test facility in Chongqing, and published plans for orbital demonstration missions. China's long-term stated goal has included a potential GW-scale SBSP system in the 2050 timeframe, though the status of near-term demonstration missions has not always been clearly reported. China's SBSP programme is one of the most active nationally, alongside those of the European Space Agency and JAXA. The programme includes both technical research and, reportedly, some ground-based system testing. However, as of the available public record, China has not launched an orbital SBSP demonstration mission. Public information about China's SBSP programme is less detailed than that of ESA or JAXA, and claims about programme status should be treated with attention to sourcing.

Answer Boundary

China's SBSP programme information is based on publicly reported statements from CAST and academic publications. Programme milestones, timelines, and current status may not be fully publicly documented. No Chinese orbital SBSP demonstration has been confirmed in the public record.
