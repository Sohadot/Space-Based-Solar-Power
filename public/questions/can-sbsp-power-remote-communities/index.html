Can SBSP power remote and off-grid communities?

Reference Question

Remote power supply is one of the use cases most frequently cited in SBSP strategic assessments, because SBSP can in principle deliver power to any location on Earth's surface that has a clear line of sight to the receiver and a small rectenna installation — without requiring the terrestrial grid infrastructure (transmission lines, substations) that serves most populations. Remote and island communities, Arctic settlements, military forward operating bases, and post-disaster areas all represent situations where grid connection is impractical or unavailable. For small remote loads, the rectenna could be far smaller and cheaper than for a utility-scale installation. This use case is attractive because it does not require competing with utility-scale electricity prices — remote diesel generation is expensive, and the comparison for SBSP is more favourable than against grid electricity. However, even for remote power delivery, an SBSP system must be in orbit — the capital cost of the space segment is shared across all use cases. No SBSP system capable of delivering power to remote communities has been deployed.

Answer Boundary

Remote power delivery is a stated use case in SBSP strategic assessments (ESA, JAXA, UK Space Energy Initiative). The economics and feasibility of SBSP for remote power have been modelled but not demonstrated. No SBSP remote power system exists.
