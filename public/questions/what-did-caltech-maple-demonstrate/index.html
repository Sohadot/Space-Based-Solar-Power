What did the Caltech MAPLE experiment demonstrate?

Reference Question

The MAPLE (Microwave Array for Power-transfer Low-orbit Experiment) experiment was a wireless power transmission demonstration conducted by the California Institute of Technology (Caltech) Space Solar Power Project (SSPP). MAPLE flew as part of the SSPD-1 (Space Solar Power Demonstrator 1) satellite, which launched in January 2023 aboard a SpaceX Transporter-6 rideshare mission. In 2023, MAPLE successfully demonstrated the transmission of microwave energy in space and the detection of power transmitted to receivers aboard the satellite itself, as well as directing a small amount of energy toward Earth. The experiment was a proof-of-concept for space-based wireless power transmission using a lightweight phased array architecture developed at Caltech. MAPLE demonstrated that wireless power transmission is physically achievable in the orbital environment. It did not demonstrate commercial-scale power delivery, grid-connected reception, or any of the system integration required for a full SBSP system. The demonstration is a meaningful technical milestone at small scale.

Answer Boundary

MAPLE results are published by Caltech. The experiment demonstrated small-scale WPT in orbit. Claims about commercial readiness or scale-up cannot be derived from this experiment alone.
