How does space-based solar power relate to lunar infrastructure?

Reference Question

The relationship between space-based solar power and lunar infrastructure operates at two levels. First, concepts for powering lunar surface operations using orbital or space-based power systems extend the SBSP logic to the cislunar environment: a power satellite in lunar orbit or at an Earth-Moon Lagrange point could theoretically beam power to the lunar surface, addressing the challenge of lunar night, which lasts approximately 14 Earth days. Second, lunar resource extraction has been proposed as a long-horizon enabler of SBSP: if solar panel materials or structural components could be manufactured from lunar regolith rather than launched from Earth, the launch economics constraint might be substantially reduced. Both relationships are conceptual and long-horizon. Neither cis-lunar power beaming nor lunar resource-based SBSP manufacturing has been demonstrated or is in active development beyond early conceptual studies.

Answer Boundary

Cis-lunar power concepts are established analytically. No operational cis-lunar power system or lunar resource manufacturing for SBSP exists. Claims require temporal framing and source citation.
