What did the NASA Space Solar Power Revisit study find?

Reference Question

The NASA Space Solar Power Revisit study, published in 2023, was commissioned to reassess SBSP concepts using modern technology assumptions — specifically improvements in solar cell efficiency, solid-state power electronics, launch vehicle reusability, and robotics. The study concluded that SBSP remains technically feasible and that advances since the 1979 reference study have improved the technical outlook. Key findings included that the mass-to-orbit requirement could be significantly reduced with modern lightweight materials and high-efficiency cells, that wireless power transmission technology had advanced, and that launch cost trajectories were more favourable than in 1979. However, the study also concluded that SBSP is not yet economically competitive with terrestrial alternatives and that significant technology development investment would be required before deployment could be considered. The study recommended continued research rather than immediate development. It did not represent a commitment to SBSP deployment by NASA.

Answer Boundary

The NASA SSPP Revisit study is a public document. Its findings represent a research assessment, not a deployment decision. Economic competitiveness conclusions depend on technology and cost assumptions that are contested.
