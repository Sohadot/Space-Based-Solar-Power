What is the technology readiness level of space-based solar power?

Reference Question

Technology readiness level (TRL) is a scale from 1 to 9 assessing how mature a technology is, from basic principles to operational deployment. For space-based solar power, TRL must be assessed component by component rather than as a single system number, because different parts of the SBSP chain are at very different maturity levels. Basic solar photovoltaic technology is mature (TRL 9 for terrestrial use). Individual rectenna elements have been demonstrated in laboratory and small field tests (TRL 5 to 7 depending on configuration). Phased array antennas are mature in radar and communications applications, but the specific scale and power levels required for SBSP have not been demonstrated (TRL 3 to 5 for SBSP-scale designs). Orbital assembly of very large structures has no demonstration at SBSP scale (TRL 2 to 3). A complete, integrated SBSP system at commercial scale would be at TRL 2 to 3 as a system, reflecting that the individual demonstrations do not constitute system-level readiness. TRL claims for SBSP must specify which component or subsystem is being assessed and at what scale.

Answer Boundary

TRL framework is established. Component-specific TRL assessments vary. Any TRL claim must specify the component, design, and source of the assessment.
