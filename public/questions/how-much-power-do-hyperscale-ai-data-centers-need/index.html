How much power do hyperscale AI data centers require?

Reference Question

Hyperscale AI data centres — the large facilities built by cloud providers and AI companies to run machine learning training and inference at scale — consume electrical power in the range of hundreds of megawatts to, in the largest planned facilities, multiple gigawatts. Existing major hyperscale data centres consume 100–500 MW. Projected AI infrastructure, if current scaling trends continue, could push individual campuses or clusters of facilities to multi-gigawatt demand. This is significant because a single large SBSP system is also typically designed to deliver 1–5 GW of power. AI infrastructure therefore represents a potential demand class that matches SBSP supply characteristics. The concentration of this power demand at specific sites also mirrors the characteristic of SBSP delivery: a focused beam to a defined location. Whether AI power demand will drive investment in SBSP is not established — current AI power demand is being met by grid connections, terrestrial renewables, and nuclear. SBSP is not currently a supply source for any data centre.

Answer Boundary

Data centre power consumption figures are derived from published company disclosures, industry reports, and analyst projections. Future AI power demand projections are uncertain. SBSP is not currently a power source for any data centre.
