What is a claim boundary and why does this site use them?

Reference Question

A claim boundary is a declared limit on what a statement, analysis, or source can responsibly assert. In SBSP analysis, claim boundaries are essential because the field combines established physics, engineering extrapolations, economic models with sensitive assumptions, strategic interpretations, and long-horizon scenarios. Without explicit claim boundaries, statements about SBSP can mislead in both directions: overstating capability and readiness in ways that create false confidence, or understating the genuine long-term potential in ways that dismiss legitimate strategic interest. This site uses claim boundaries as a governance mechanism. Every technical claim, economic projection, and strategic argument is accompanied by a statement defining the basis and limits of the claim: what is established, what is modeled, what is demonstrated, and what remains uncertain. This approach serves researchers, journalists, investors, and governments who need to understand not just what is claimed, but how confident the underlying evidence allows us to be. A site that presents SBSP claims without boundaries is a site that cannot be trusted as a reference.

Answer Boundary
