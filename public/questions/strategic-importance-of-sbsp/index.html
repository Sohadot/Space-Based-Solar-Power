What is the strategic importance of space-based solar power?

Reference Question

The strategic importance of space-based solar power lies primarily in what it represents as a long-horizon capability rather than a current operational system. SBSP matters strategically for several reasons. Energy sovereignty: nations developing SBSP capability would gain access to a power source independent of geographic, weather, and fuel supply constraints. Grid resilience: SBSP could theoretically contribute power independent of terrestrial infrastructure vulnerabilities. Defense logistics: wireless power delivery could reduce military fuel supply chain exposure. AI and advanced computing energy demand: SBSP is cited as a potential very long-horizon supply option as compute energy demand grows. Space-industrial development: SBSP development would drive advances in orbital assembly, wireless power transmission, and large-scale space infrastructure relevant to many future space activities. The strategic case does not depend on near-term commercial deployment. Governments and space agencies maintain SBSP research interest partly because the technology development pathway also strengthens broader space industrial capabilities with multiple strategic applications.

Answer Boundary

Strategic arguments are analytical. None of the strategic contributions cited represent current operational capability. All require long-horizon temporal framing.
