What is public legitimacy as a constraint for SBSP?

Reference Question

Public legitimacy refers to the degree of public understanding, acceptance, and trust that a technology or infrastructure project requires to proceed. For SBSP, public legitimacy is a constraint because the deployment of a system that transmits microwave energy from space to large ground sites would require community acceptance in areas where rectenna fields are sited, and broader societal confidence that the technology is safe and appropriately governed. Public unfamiliarity with space-based energy systems, concerns about electromagnetic radiation, and questions about land use for rectenna fields create potential legitimacy gaps that must be addressed through transparent communication, demonstrated safety, and inclusive planning processes. Institutional SBSP studies — including ESA's Solaris assessment — have identified public acceptance as a constraint that must be addressed alongside technical and economic barriers. Historical infrastructure projects involving large land footprints or electromagnetic emissions have faced public opposition that has delayed or prevented deployment. SBSP proponents argue that transparency about safety data and community consultation can build the necessary public legitimacy, but this has not been tested at scale.

Answer Boundary

Public legitimacy as a constraint is identified in SBSP feasibility assessments. No SBSP system has faced a real public legitimacy process, as no system has reached deployment stage. Historical analogies from other infrastructure projects are informative but not directly applicable.
