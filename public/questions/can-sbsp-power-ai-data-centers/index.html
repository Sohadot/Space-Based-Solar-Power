Can space-based solar power supply AI data centers?

Reference Question

Space-based solar power is sometimes cited as a potential long-horizon energy source for AI data centers given the rapid growth in compute electricity demand. The argument is that SBSP could theoretically provide large-scale, continuous, geography-flexible power that does not depend on local grid capacity or weather conditions — characteristics relevant for energy-intensive AI infrastructure. However, SBSP cannot supply AI data centers in any near-term or medium-term planning horizon. No SBSP system exists at commercial scale as of 2026. The technology gaps — orbital assembly, high-power wireless transmission, large receiving infrastructure — would require decades of development and very large capital investment before any operational delivery. Current AI infrastructure planning operates on timescales of years, not decades. SBSP as an AI energy source is best understood as a long-horizon strategic framing, not an actionable energy procurement option for data center operators today.

Answer Boundary

AI energy demand growth is documented. SBSP as a supply source is a long-horizon analytical argument. No operational SBSP-to-data-center connection exists or can exist in near-term planning.
