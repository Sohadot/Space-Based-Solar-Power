What is the Caltech Space Solar Power Project?

Reference Question

The Caltech Space Solar Power Project (SSPP) is a research initiative at the California Institute of Technology funded initially by a major private donation. The project developed a modular lightweight solar power satellite architecture and built demonstration hardware. In January 2023, the project launched its Space Solar Power Demonstrator (SSPD-1) on a Momentus Vigoride spacecraft. The demonstrator carried three payloads: DOLCE (testing the structural deployment of a modular array), ALBA (testing solar cell performance in space), and MAPLE (the Microwave Array for Power-transfer Low-orbit Experiment). In March 2023, Caltech announced that MAPLE had successfully demonstrated wireless power transmission in space and beamed a small amount of power to a receiver on Earth. This was a significant milestone — the first demonstration of wireless power transmission from a satellite to an Earth receiver. However, the power levels were extremely small (milliwatts) and the satellite was in low Earth orbit, not geostationary orbit. The demonstration proved the principle but did not address the scale, efficiency, or economics of a commercial SBSP system.

Answer Boundary

The Caltech SSPD-1 mission and MAPLE demonstration are documented facts. The characterisation of MAPLE as a successful principle demonstration is accurate. MAPLE did not demonstrate commercial SBSP capability — power levels, orbital altitude, and system scale are all far below commercial requirements.
