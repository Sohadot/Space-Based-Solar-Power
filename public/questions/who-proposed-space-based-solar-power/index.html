Who proposed space-based solar power and when?

Reference Question

Space-based solar power was formally proposed by aerospace engineer Peter Glaser of Arthur D. Little, Inc. in a paper titled 'Power from the Sun: Its Future,' published in Science magazine in 1968. Glaser described a large solar array in geostationary orbit that would collect solar energy, convert it to microwave radiation, and transmit it to a ground-based receiving station equipped with a rectifying antenna array. The paper established the basic architecture that most subsequent SBSP research has followed. Glaser was not the first person to think conceptually about orbital solar power, but his 1968 paper is the recognized foundational reference in the field. The concept subsequently attracted serious institutional attention in the 1970s, leading to the major NASA/DOE Reference System Study of 1979. Glaser's work was recognized with several awards, and the concept continues to bear his mark in the literature. The 1968 paper is a historical document; its technical specifications and economic assumptions reflect 1960s technology and cost structures.

Answer Boundary

The 1968 Glaser paper is a documented historical fact. It is a foundational reference, not a current technical specification.
