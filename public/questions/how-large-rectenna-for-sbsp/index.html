How large does a rectenna need to be for a space-based solar power system?

Reference Question

The required size of a rectenna receiving array for an SBSP system depends on several factors: the power level being transmitted, the microwave frequency used, the altitude and orbital position of the satellite, and the acceptable power density at the rectenna surface. Because microwave beams spread as they propagate over the roughly 36,000 km from geostationary orbit, the receiving area must be large enough to capture the diffracted beam. Early reference designs, including the 1979 NASA study, proposed rectennas on the order of 10 km by 13 km for a system delivering approximately 5 GW to the grid. More recent designs propose various alternatives, including higher frequencies (which reduce required aperture size), lower power levels, or offshore placement. Estimates across different published studies range from tens to hundreds of square kilometers depending on the design parameters assumed. There is no single standard rectenna size: the figure is always design-specific, and any quoted value must be linked to the design study it comes from.

Answer Boundary

Physical beam propagation is established physics. Specific area estimates are design-dependent. Any quoted figure must reference its source design study and assumptions.
