What is space industrial energy and how does SBSP relate to it?

Reference Question

Space industrial energy refers to the electrical power required to support an in-space industrial economy: orbital manufacturing facilities, propellant production, resource processing from asteroidal or lunar materials, and large-scale habitation. As space activity scales toward a commercial space economy, energy in space becomes a primary infrastructure requirement — analogous to the electricity grid on Earth. SBSP technology is foundational to this concept: large orbital solar arrays, power distribution and conversion in the space environment, and power beaming between space assets all derive from the same technology base as terrestrial SBSP. An SBSP system built for Earth power delivery could, in principle, also supply power to other orbital assets. Conversely, in-space industrial activity that develops power generation capability for its own purposes could progressively enable SBSP. JAXA's long-term SSPS roadmap and NASA's space solar power concepts include this broader energy economy vision. Space industrial energy is currently a strategic framing concept, not an operational sector.

Answer Boundary

Space industrial energy is a strategic framing concept addressed in institutional roadmaps (JAXA, NASA, academic). No space industrial energy economy exists. The technology foundations are at research and early-demonstration stage.
