What is cislunar energy infrastructure and why does SBSP matter for it?

Reference Question

Cislunar space refers to the volume of space between Earth and the Moon, including lunar orbit. As national and commercial space programmes expand, cislunar space is becoming an active operational environment: lunar surface missions, gateways in lunar orbit, and transit routes between Earth orbit and the Moon all require energy. SBSP technology — specifically the expertise in large orbital solar arrays and power beaming — is directly relevant to powering cislunar infrastructure. A solar power satellite positioned at a cislunar location could provide power to a lunar surface base without the mass burden of carrying fuel or batteries to the Moon. Power beaming techniques developed for SBSP could transmit energy to spacecraft or surface assets in shadow. The development of SBSP for terrestrial power delivery and the development of cislunar power systems share technology foundations. Strategic assessments from JAXA and others position terrestrial SBSP as a step toward a broader space power economy that includes cislunar and eventually interplanetary energy infrastructure.

Answer Boundary

Cislunar energy infrastructure is a stated strategic context for SBSP in institutional assessments (JAXA, NASA, academic studies). No cislunar SBSP power system has been deployed. The technology foundations are shared with terrestrial SBSP but the applications are distinct.
