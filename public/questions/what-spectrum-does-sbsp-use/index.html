What radio frequency spectrum does SBSP use and who regulates it?

Reference Question

SBSP designs most commonly propose operating at 2.45 GHz or 5.8 GHz — frequencies in the Industrial, Scientific and Medical (ISM) bands that are internationally allocated and already used for devices such as microwave ovens and Wi-Fi. These frequencies are favoured for SBSP because of their relatively low atmospheric attenuation and the existing technology base for transmitters and rectifiers. Radio frequency spectrum is allocated and regulated internationally by the International Telecommunication Union (ITU) and nationally by bodies such as the FCC in the United States, Ofcom in the United Kingdom, and equivalent agencies in other countries. An operational SBSP system transmitting at gigawatt power levels in these bands would require specific regulatory authorisation, interference analysis, and likely new international treaty arrangements — because the power levels involved are far above any existing ISM band usage. The spectrum regulatory pathway for SBSP has not been established. This is identified as a governance constraint in SBSP feasibility assessments.

Answer Boundary

Spectrum allocation facts are based on current ITU and national regulatory frameworks. The regulatory pathway for SBSP operation has not been established — it is a stated governance constraint, not a resolved question.
