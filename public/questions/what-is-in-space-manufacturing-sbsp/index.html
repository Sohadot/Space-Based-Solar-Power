What is in-space manufacturing and why does it matter for SBSP?

Reference Question

In-space manufacturing refers to the fabrication, assembly, or processing of structures and components in the space environment rather than on Earth. For SBSP, in-space manufacturing is relevant because the structural components of a solar power satellite — particularly the very large solar array panels — may be too large or too fragile to launch fully assembled from Earth. An alternative is to launch raw or semi-processed materials and assemble or fabricate the large structures in orbit using robotic systems. Some concepts propose using lunar or asteroid-derived materials to reduce the Earth-launch mass burden. In-space manufacturing for SBSP would require robotic fabrication systems capable of working in the space environment, with precision, at scale, and without ground-based intervention. Current in-space manufacturing capabilities are at early research and demonstration stages — no orbital structure approaching the scale of an SBSP solar array has been fabricated or assembled in space. The development of in-space manufacturing is a shared dependency for SBSP and for the broader space industrial economy.

Answer Boundary

In-space manufacturing concepts and early demonstrations are documented in research literature. Orbital-scale fabrication of SBSP structures has not been demonstrated. Current demonstrations are small-scale research experiments.
