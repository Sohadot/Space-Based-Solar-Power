What is the UK Space Energy Initiative?

Reference Question

The UK Space Energy Initiative (SEI) is a UK government-backed programme that conducted a feasibility study of space-based solar power as a potential contribution to the UK's net-zero energy goals. The initiative, which involved industry, research institutions, and government, published its Cassini report in 2021 and a more detailed assessment in 2023. The studies concluded that SBSP was technically feasible and could potentially be cost-competitive with other low-carbon energy sources if launch costs continue to fall, but that it would require sustained public investment in research and development to progress toward deployment. The UK government's position as of the study period was that SBSP warranted continued assessment rather than immediate commitment to deployment. The initiative gave SBSP higher visibility in UK energy and space policy discussions and has contributed to the international framing of SBSP as a strategic energy option rather than a speculative curiosity. No deployment decision has been made by the UK government.

Answer Boundary

The UK Space Energy Initiative reports are public institutional documents. Their conclusions represent assessments, not deployment commitments. No UK SBSP system has been funded for deployment.
