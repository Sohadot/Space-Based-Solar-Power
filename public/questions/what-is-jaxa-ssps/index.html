What is JAXA's role in space-based solar power research?

Reference Question

JAXA (Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency) has maintained one of the world's longest-running and most sustained institutional SBSP research programs. Japanese SBSP research dates to the 1980s, with JAXA conducting technical studies, engineering research, and wireless power transmission demonstrations over several decades. In 2015, JAXA conducted a notable ground demonstration of microwave wireless power transmission, delivering kilowatt-scale power over a distance of approximately 55 meters using a phased array transmitter and a rectenna receiver. JAXA has published long-term roadmaps for SBSP development, with aspirational targets for orbital demonstrations and eventual commercial deployment extending into the 2030s and beyond. These roadmaps are planning documents reflecting research targets, not funded commitments. JAXA's sustained interest has provided a significant body of technical documentation on SBSP system design, wireless power transmission, and orbital system concepts.

Answer Boundary

JAXA program history and demonstrations are documented. Roadmap timelines are planning targets, not commitments. Current program status requires current JAXA source verification.
