Can SBSP deliver power during disaster response and emergencies?

Reference Question

Disaster response power is a strategic use case for SBSP that several institutional assessments highlight, particularly those with a defence or resilience focus. In disaster scenarios — earthquakes, hurricanes, major grid failures — terrestrial electricity infrastructure is often damaged or destroyed. SBSP could, in principle, direct a power beam to a portable rectenna deployed at the disaster site, providing electricity without needing connection to the damaged grid. The beam could be redirected from its normal delivery location to the emergency site in a matter of hours. This is sometimes called the beamed emergency power delivery concept. The military dimensions are related: forward operating bases, disaster relief operations, and humanitarian missions all have power needs in locations without grid access. The UK Space Energy Initiative and JAXA SSPS assessments explicitly list resilience and emergency power delivery as strategic rationales for SBSP investment. However, no such disaster response power delivery has ever been demonstrated — the concept requires a working SBSP system as a precondition.

Answer Boundary

Disaster response power delivery is a stated use case in institutional SBSP assessments. No SBSP emergency power system has been deployed or tested. The scenario requires a working orbital SBSP system, which does not exist.
