This registry governs the evidence base for Space-Based-Solar-Power.com. Each source record documents authority role, claim scope, verification posture, and explicit boundary notes. Sources are classified into seven institutional categories. No source is treated as unconditional authority — all are bounded by what they can and cannot establish.

How sources are used

Sources are applied according to a three-tier hierarchy. Tier 1 sources (institutional space agencies, government policy) establish the primary reference baseline. Tier 2 sources (academic research, technical standards) provide analytical and experimental grounding. Tier 3 sources (industry and consortium) are used only for documenting institutional activity and company claims — never to establish independent technical claims. Internal methodology sources govern editorial practice, not external claims.

Claim boundaries on every page specify which source tier supports each category of claim, and where claims remain unresolved, contested, or dependent on assumptions. See Methodology for the full claim classification framework.

Source governance and claim boundaries

Every page on this site carries an explicit claim boundary statement. That statement identifies which claims are source-verified, which are institutional summaries, which are technical explanations with modelled assumptions, which are company claims requiring independent verification, and which remain genuinely unresolved.

No source record in this registry is used to assert that space-based solar power is commercially deployed, operational at utility scale, or a proven full infrastructure system. The current state of SBSP is pre-deployment: technology components are at varying readiness levels, programmes are in feasibility study or early demonstration phases, and no operational system exists as of the date of this page.

Institutional Space Agency Sources

  • NASA 1979 Reference System Study — foundational technical baseline

    Establishes the original system architecture for satellite solar power, including microwave power transmission frequency, orbital parameters, ground receiver (rectenna) design, and initial mass and cost estimates. Defines the baseline reference configuration that subsequent studies have built on or revised.

    Boundary: Does not validate current technical feasibility. Predates modern phased-array transmitters, thin-film solar cells, significant launch cost reductions, and in-space assembly robotics. Must be cited as historical reference with explicit qualification that assumptions have changed materially since 1979.

  • ESA SOLARIS Concurrent Design Facility Assessment 2022 — active institutional feasibility study

    Documents ESA's internal assessment of SBSP feasibility under the SOLARIS initiative, covering system architecture options, wireless power transmission approaches, required technology development, cost estimates, and recommended further study phases.

    Boundary: SOLARIS is a feasibility study phase, not an approved programme. Does not confirm commercial deployment, operational system, or funded mission. Timelines cited are study assumptions, not funded milestones.

  • JAXA Space Solar Power Systems Conceptual Study — long-term institutional research reference

    Documents JAXA's SSPS conceptual studies including system design, WPT experiments, structural concepts, and roadmap targets for SPS2030+ and SPS2050 technology development phases.

    Boundary: Roadmap dates are planning targets, not funded commitments. Ground-based WPT demonstrations validate component technology, not full system deployment.

  • ISRO Space-Based Solar Power Feasibility Study — national agency feasibility assessment

    Documents ISRO's internal assessment of SBSP feasibility and India's potential role in SBSP development, including launch infrastructure considerations and technology development requirements.

    Boundary: Study phase only. Does not represent a funded SBSP programme or confirmed deployment trajectory for India.

Government Policy and Commissioned Studies

  • Frazer-Nash Consultancy SBSP Feasibility Study (UK BEIS, 2020) — government-commissioned feasibility study

    UK government-commissioned independent feasibility study covering system options, costs, technical readiness, manufacturing scale requirements, and policy recommendations for a UK SBSP programme. Primary source for UK strategic positioning and cost modelling.

    Boundary: Cost estimates are modelling outputs with stated assumptions, not procurement figures. Commissioning does not represent a UK government SBSP deployment decision.

  • US Department of Defense SBSP Energy Logistics Study — defence energy strategy reference

    Documents US DoD and AFRL interest in SBSP as a forward-deployed energy logistics solution, covering military energy resilience applications, SSPIDR demonstration programme context, and strategic rationale for defence-driven SBSP investment.

    Boundary: Strategic interest in energy logistics does not confirm near-term SBSP deployment. Demonstration programme (SSPIDR) is technology validation, not system deployment.

  • European Union Space-Based Solar Power Initiative Documentation — multi-nation policy coordination reference

    Documents EU-level interest and coordination on SBSP across member states, including the SPS ESCAPE concept, EU space industrial strategy, and policy alignment with energy security and net-zero objectives.

    Boundary: EU coordination does not constitute an approved programme. Policy alignment with energy goals is intent, not funded project.

Academic and Peer-Reviewed Research

  • Caltech Space Solar Power Project Research Publications (Hajimiri et al.) — peer-reviewed demonstration research

    Peer-reviewed publications documenting Caltech SSPP system design, MAPLE experiment design and in-space results (2023), integrated photovoltaic-transmitter tile development, and ultralight flexible structural concepts.

    Boundary: MAPLE demonstrated targeted wireless power transfer in space at small scale. This confirms component-level TRL progression, not full system feasibility or commercial viability at utility scale.

  • MIT Space-Based Solar Power Research — academic systems analysis reference

    MIT research covering SBSP system-level analysis, techno-economic modelling, rectenna array design, and launch cost sensitivity studies. Contributes to published academic consensus on SBSP feasibility conditions.

    Boundary: Systems analysis and modelling — conclusions are conditional on technology and cost assumptions. Does not confirm commercial deployment viability.

  • University of Cambridge SBSP Engineering Research — structural and systems engineering reference

    Cambridge research addressing large-structure dynamics, orbital assembly, attitude control, and thermal management for SBSP systems. Published in peer-reviewed engineering journals.

    Boundary: Engineering analyses are conditional on design assumptions. Does not validate full system performance in operational conditions.

  • Georgia Tech Space-Based Solar Power Research — power systems and architecture research reference

    Georgia Tech research on SBSP power system architecture, microwave power beaming efficiency, rectenna design, and system-level cost optimisation.

    Boundary: Laboratory-scale results. Does not confirm system-level performance or commercial deployment feasibility.

  • Kyushu University Space Solar Power Research — academic WPT and photovoltaics research reference

    Kyushu University research contributions to SBSP subsystem development including high-efficiency photovoltaics for space, WPT efficiency improvements, and collaborative work with JAXA.

    Boundary: Subsystem research. Does not confirm full-system SBSP deployment feasibility.

  • Tsinghua University Space-Based Solar Power Research — Chinese academic SBSP systems reference

    Tsinghua University research on SBSP system design, large-structure orbital deployment, and WPT, aligned with China's national SBSP programme strategy.

    Boundary: Academic research supporting national programme goals. Does not confirm deployment commitments or operational system timelines.

Technical Standards and Regulatory References

  • IEEE Wireless Power Transfer Standards and Technical References — engineering standards body reference

    IEEE technical standards, working group reports, and published reviews covering WPT efficiency benchmarks, frequency allocations, safety exposure limits, and system performance characterisation methods applicable to SBSP microwave transmission.

    Boundary: Standards apply to defined conditions. Space-to-ground WPT at SBSP scale introduces scenarios not fully covered by existing standards — regulatory development is ongoing.

  • ITU Radio Spectrum Regulations and WRC Documentation — international regulatory authority

    ITU Radio Regulations, World Radiocommunication Conference (WRC) documentation, and ITU-R study group outputs relevant to frequency allocation for SBSP microwave power transmission.

    Boundary: Dedicated SBSP spectrum allocation has not been formally agreed at ITU level as of 2024–2025. Current regulatory framework represents a constraint requiring resolution before operational SBSP deployment.

Industry and Consortium Sources

  • Airbus Space-Based Solar Power Concept Studies — industry concept and capability reference

    Airbus internal and published concept studies on SBSP system architecture, manufacturing approaches, and potential industrial roles for a European SBSP programme, including collaboration with ESA SOLARIS.

    Boundary: Industry concept work. Does not represent a funded development contract or confirmed deployment commitment. Cost and schedule claims are company planning assumptions.

  • Thales Alenia Space SBSP Research and Concept Work — space systems industry reference

    Thales Alenia Space contributions to SBSP system architecture, reflector/concentrator concepts, and participation in ESA and European industry SBSP activities.

    Boundary: Concept and study phase contributions. Not a funded product or programme commitment.

  • Northrop Grumman SSPIDR Programme Documentation — defence industry demonstration reference

    Documents the Space Solar Power Incremental Demonstrations and Research (SSPIDR) programme, including the ARACHNE flight experiment design, phased array transmitter development, and in-space WPT demonstration objectives.

    Boundary: Demonstration programme context. SSPIDR validates component and subsystem technologies, not full SBSP system deployment.

  • Mitsubishi Electric Wireless Power Transmission Research — industry WPT component research reference

    Mitsubishi Electric published research and demonstrations on high-efficiency microwave WPT, phased array transmitters, and ground rectenna systems, in support of JAXA SSPS programme.

    Boundary: Component and subsystem demonstrations. Does not represent operational space-to-ground WPT at deployment scale.

  • IHI Corporation Space-Based Solar Power Research — Japanese industrial SBSP reference

    IHI Corporation research on SBSP structural systems, large-space-structure assembly, and solar array deployment, contributing to Japan's national SBSP industrial development base.

    Boundary: Industrial research and development phase. Does not represent a funded deployment contract.

Historical and Foundational References

  • Peter Glaser 1968 Science Paper — Space Solar Power Original Concept — foundational concept origin reference

    The original published concept for space-based solar power by Peter Glaser (Science, 1968), proposing a satellite solar power station with microwave transmission to a ground receiving antenna. Establishes the conceptual foundation for all subsequent SBSP studies.

    Boundary: 1968 concept paper. Technology assumptions, cost estimates, and system specifications are entirely historical. Must be cited as the origin concept, not as current engineering guidance.

  • NASA / DoE 1980 Concept Development and Evaluation Programme — historical programme baseline reference

    The NASA/Department of Energy joint Concept Development and Evaluation Program (CDEP) that produced detailed engineering studies of SBSP from 1976–1980, including the Reference System and numerous alternative concept assessments.

    Boundary: Late 1970s programme. Technology assumptions pre-date modern launch vehicles, phased arrays, thin-film photovoltaics, and robotic assembly systems. Use as historical baseline only.

  • NASA Fresh Look Study 1995–1997 (Mankins) — mid-period reassessment reference

    NASA's internal re-evaluation of SBSP feasibility in 1995–1997, led by John Mankins, assessing whether advances since the 1979 study changed the feasibility picture. Introduced the SPS Alpha concept and proposed an updated development pathway.

    Boundary: 1995 reassessment. Technology assumptions have continued to evolve significantly since 1997. Must be framed as a historical reassessment, not current engineering guidance.
