The US Naval Research Laboratory has a long history of wireless power transmission research relevant to space-based solar power, including foundational work on microwave power beaming, rectenna technology, and power conversion efficiency. NRL's WPT research conducted from the 1960s through the 1980s provided critical experimental data supporting the feasibility of microwave wireless power transmission at distances and efficiencies relevant to SBSP concepts. This foundational research established key physical constraints and demonstrated early rectenna conversion efficiencies that underpinned subsequent SBSP feasibility assessments including the 1979 NASA reference system study.

Institutional Context

The US Naval Research Laboratory conducted WPT research as part of broader defense interest in advanced power systems and directed energy. NRL researchers including William Brown made foundational contributions to rectenna technology and microwave power beaming efficiency. NRL's work was directly cited in the 1979 NASA SBSP reference system study and contributed to the first generation of serious SBSP feasibility analysis.

SBSP Relevance

NRL's foundational WPT research is historically significant for establishing the physical and engineering basis for microwave power beaming at SBSP-relevant parameters. NRL rectenna efficiency demonstrations and microwave beaming experiments remain references in the SBSP technical literature and establish the foundational feasibility evidence for wireless power transmission as an SBSP subsystem.

Technology Relationship

NRL WPT research is directly relevant to microwave power transmission, rectenna arrays, phased array transmitter systems, and power conversion and conditioning as primary technology domains. NRL's historical experiments provide early TRL evidence for these subsystems.

Feasibility Boundary

NRL's historical WPT research demonstrated subsystem-level feasibility at laboratory and outdoor demonstration scale. Demonstrations from this period do not resolve system-level integration, orbital deployment, or cost feasibility for a complete SBSP system.

Claim Boundary

NRL's historical WPT research should be cited as foundational experimental evidence for microwave WPT subsystem feasibility, not as demonstration of complete SBSP system feasibility. Historical demonstrations at kilowatt scale do not imply readiness for gigawatt-scale orbital deployment.
