Northrop Grumman's Space Solar Power Incremental Demonstrations and Research (SSPIDR) programme is a US defense-funded industry research and demonstration effort aimed at developing key technology components for space-based wireless power transmission. SSPIDR is developing modular photovoltaic and microwave transmission tile technologies intended to demonstrate power beaming from orbit. The programme represents a significant US government investment in SBSP-relevant technology demonstration and positions Northrop Grumman as a primary US industrial actor in the SBSP technology development space. SSPIDR is a technology demonstration programme, not a commitment to deploy an operational space-based solar power system.

Institutional Context

SSPIDR is funded by AFRL and represents a collaboration between the US Air Force, Northrop Grumman, and associated industrial and academic partners. The programme builds on prior AFRL power beaming research and targets the demonstration of modular tile-based photovoltaic conversion and microwave transmission at orbital scale. SSPIDR is part of the broader US defense investment in advanced power and energy technologies.

SBSP Relevance

SSPIDR is the most advanced US defense-linked SBSP technology demonstration programme currently active. Its focus on modular tile-based architecture, which is directly compatible with the modular spacecraft architecture concepts central to contemporary SBSP system design, makes it a highly relevant technology development reference. SSPIDR's demonstration results will contribute to TRL advancement for key SBSP subsystems.

Technology Relationship

SSPIDR development is directly focused on phased array transmitter systems, modular spacecraft architecture, high-efficiency solar cells, power conversion and conditioning, and microwave power transmission. The modular tile concept is a primary architectural innovation with direct relevance to scalable SBSP system design.

Feasibility Boundary

SSPIDR is a technology demonstration programme at component and prototype scale. Demonstration of modular tile performance at laboratory or orbital scale does not constitute demonstration of a full operational SBSP system. The gap between tile-level demonstration and gigawatt-scale operational deployment remains the primary feasibility boundary.

Claim Boundary

SSPIDR results should be described as technology component demonstrations, not as proof of SBSP commercial or operational feasibility. Successful tile demonstrations advance TRL for specific subsystems but do not resolve system-level integration, mass, cost, and deployment challenges.
