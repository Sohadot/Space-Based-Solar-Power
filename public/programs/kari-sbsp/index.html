The Korea Aerospace Research Institute has examined space-based solar power as part of South Korea's long-range space technology planning. KARI's SBSP study activities reflect South Korea's dual interest in advancing its space programme capabilities and addressing national energy security through technology diversification. KARI contributes to international SBSP research discourse and represents the expansion of serious institutional engagement with SBSP beyond the original proponent nations. No funded Korean SBSP development programme has been publicly confirmed.

Institutional Context

KARI is South Korea's national space agency and conducts research across satellite, launch, and space technology domains. South Korea's strong semiconductor and electronics manufacturing base gives it relevant industrial capacity for SBSP component development, particularly in photovoltaics and power electronics. KARI's SBSP engagement occurs within the context of South Korea's broader space programme ambitions and national energy policy.

SBSP Relevance

KARI's engagement with SBSP is significant as an indicator of the technology's emergence as a serious research topic across the Asia-Pacific space technology community. South Korea's industrial strengths in electronics and photovoltaics make it a potentially relevant contributor to future SBSP technology supply chains.

Technology Relationship

KARI SBSP studies engage with high-efficiency solar cells, power conversion and conditioning, and wireless power transmission as primary technology domains relevant to South Korea's industrial capabilities.

Feasibility Boundary

KARI SBSP activities are study-phase engagements, not funded development programmes. No Korean national SBSP development commitment has been publicly announced.

Claim Boundary

KARI's SBSP study activities do not represent a Korean national commitment to develop space-based solar power. Institutional interest at study phase should not be represented as a funded programme or near-term deployment plan.
