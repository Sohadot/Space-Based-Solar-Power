European Union-level space power concept studies encompass research activities coordinated through the European Commission and ESA member state institutions examining the potential role of space-based solar power in European energy strategy. These studies are distinct from the ESA SOLARIS initiative in representing broader EU policy-level engagement with SBSP as an energy security and strategic technology question. EU-level engagement reflects the intersection of European space policy, energy transition goals, and industrial competitiveness considerations. No EU-level funded SBSP development programme has been established.

Institutional Context

EU space power concept studies draw on research from member state institutions across Germany, France, Italy, Spain, and other ESA member states, coordinated through European Commission research funding mechanisms and ESA's member state engagement. The European Green Deal and REPowerEU energy security framework provide the policy context for EU-level SBSP interest.

SBSP Relevance

EU-level SBSP concept engagement is relevant to understanding the policy-level institutional landscape for SBSP in Europe. EU research funding and industrial policy instruments could play a significant role in any future European SBSP development programme, making EU-level institutional engagement a key factor in the European SBSP trajectory.

Technology Relationship

EU space power concept studies engage with the full SBSP technology spectrum, including solar collection, wireless power transmission, in-space assembly, and ground receiving systems, within the context of European industrial capability assessment.

Feasibility Boundary

EU space power concept studies are pre-decisional policy research activities. No EU-level funded SBSP development programme has been established. All outputs are study-phase inputs to European energy and space policy deliberations.

Claim Boundary

EU-level SBSP concept engagement does not represent a European Union commitment to develop or fund a space-based solar power system. Study outputs are pre-decisional policy research findings, not development mandates.
