ESA SOLARIS is a preparatory initiative launched by the European Space Agency to assess the feasibility of space-based solar power for European energy security. The initiative is evaluating system architectures, technology readiness levels, and policy frameworks. SOLARIS represents one of the most formally structured space agency feasibility programmes currently active globally, with a mandate to inform whether ESA member states should pursue a full development commitment. No deployment decision has been made and the initiative remains in a pre-decision study phase.

Institutional Context

ESA SOLARIS was formally proposed to ESA's Ministerial Council and received study mandates from ESA's directorate structure. It operates within the context of European energy security concerns following geopolitical disruptions to conventional energy supply chains. The initiative draws on expertise across ESA member state research institutions and engages industrial partners for architecture and cost assessments.

SBSP Relevance

SOLARIS is one of the highest-profile active feasibility studies in the global SBSP landscape. It is directly relevant to understanding the current institutional momentum behind space-based solar power research and the conditions under which a major space agency might recommend a development commitment. Its scope, methodology, and outputs are reference points for the entire European SBSP research ecosystem.

Technology Relationship

SOLARIS assessments span the full SBSP technology chain, including high-efficiency photovoltaic conversion, microwave wireless power transmission, phased array transmitter systems, in-space assembly, and ground receiving infrastructure. Technology readiness evaluations are a core component of the initiative's mandate, with TRL assessments covering all major subsystems.

Feasibility Boundary

SOLARIS is a study initiative, not a development programme. All outputs are pre-decisional assessments. No launch or deployment schedule exists. The programme's role is to determine whether a development commitment would be justified, not to commit to one.

Claim Boundary

No claim should be made that ESA has committed to building a space-based solar power system. SOLARIS outputs represent study-phase findings, not funded development milestones. TRL assessments from SOLARIS reflect current research status and should not be interpreted as readiness for deployment.
