Georgia Institute of Technology has engaged with space-based solar power research through contributions to systems architecture analysis, microwave power transmission technology, and SBSP economic assessment. Georgia Tech researchers have published on SBSP system design, technology readiness gaps, and the engineering challenges of large-scale space power systems. Georgia Tech's SBSP research is conducted within the broader context of the institution's strong aerospace and electrical engineering research programmes and contributes to the US academic knowledge base on SBSP technical feasibility and economic constraints.

Institutional Context

Georgia Tech is one of the leading US engineering universities with strong research programmes in aerospace, electrical engineering, and energy systems. Georgia Tech researchers engage with the US SBSP research community through publications, conference participation, and collaboration with government research agencies. The institution's engineering focus makes it particularly relevant to the technical assessment aspects of SBSP feasibility.

SBSP Relevance

Georgia Tech's SBSP research contributions are relevant to the US academic knowledge base on SBSP technical feasibility and economic analysis. Georgia Tech's systems engineering expertise is applicable to the complex multi-subsystem integration challenges central to SBSP feasibility assessment.

Technology Relationship

Georgia Tech SBSP research engages with microwave power transmission, phased array transmitter systems, modular spacecraft architecture, and in-space assembly as primary technology domains relevant to the institution's research strengths.

Feasibility Boundary

Georgia Tech's SBSP research activities are academic research and analysis. No Georgia Tech-led SBSP development programme or demonstration facility exists.

Claim Boundary

Georgia Tech SBSP research should be cited as academic research contributions to the US SBSP knowledge base, not as development commitments or demonstrations of operational capability.
