Defense Military

The US Department of Defense has conducted and sponsored studies examining the potential role of space-based solar power and wireless power transmission in defense energy logistics, forward operating base resupply, and military energy resilience. DoD interest in SBSP reflects the strategic vulnerability of fuel supply chains in contested environments and the potential for space-based power delivery to reduce logistics burden and increase operational resilience. These studies encompass both near-term power beaming applications and longer-range SBSP system concepts. DoD engagement with SBSP is a distinct dimension of the technology's strategic relevance beyond civilian energy applications.

Institutional Context

DoD energy logistics studies draw on expertise from DARPA, AFRL, the Defense Innovation Unit, and military service research organisations. The strategic driver is the high cost and vulnerability of fuel logistics for remote and forward-deployed military operations. Space-based power delivery concepts are evaluated within the DoD's broader energy security and operational resilience framework.

SBSP Relevance

DoD's strategic interest in SBSP and wireless power transmission is a significant dimension of the technology's broader relevance landscape. Military energy resilience applications could serve as an early-adoption pathway for SBSP-related technologies, providing funding and operational experience that builds toward broader SBSP capability. DoD studies also inform the US government's overall understanding of SBSP strategic value.

Technology Relationship

DoD SBSP energy logistics studies engage with wireless power transmission, phased array transmitter systems, power conversion and conditioning, and defense energy resilience as primary technology domains. Near-term applications focus on terrestrial power beaming demonstrations relevant to military operational scenarios.

Feasibility Boundary

DoD SBSP energy logistics studies are strategic planning and technology assessment activities. No DoD orbital SBSP deployment programme has been confirmed. Near-term power beaming demonstrations are at early TRL levels and do not constitute operational capability.

Claim Boundary

DoD interest in SBSP-related technologies should not be presented as a military commitment to deploy space-based solar power. Strategic interest and technology exploration are distinct from programmed acquisition. All DoD SBSP-related activities are at research and study phase.
