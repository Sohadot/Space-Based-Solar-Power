National Research Program

China has one of the most active national space-based solar power research and demonstration programmes globally, encompassing strategic planning at the national level, dedicated research facilities, and ground demonstration experiments. The China Academy of Space Technology and associated institutions have conducted wireless power transmission experiments and published roadmap targets for orbital demonstration and eventual operational phases. China's SBSP programme is supported by national energy security and space power strategic priorities. Ground demonstration phases have been conducted, but no orbital SBSP demonstration mission has been confirmed as of this reference layer's knowledge boundary.

Institutional Context

China's SBSP research is embedded within the national space programme and energy strategy. The Chongqing Collaborative Research Centre for Space Solar Power has been a primary institutional locus for ground demonstration and system integration research. China's SBSP activities have received national-level policy attention, reflecting SBSP's role in China's long-range energy and space technology planning. Chinese research publications on SBSP architecture, WPT demonstrations, and cost modelling contribute to the global knowledge base.

SBSP Relevance

China's SBSP programme represents the most advanced national programme in terms of ground demonstration scale and stated policy commitment, though the gap between ground demonstration and orbital deployment remains large. China's programme is a critical reference point for assessing the global competitive landscape of SBSP development and the range of national strategies being pursued.

Technology Relationship

Chinese SBSP research engages with microwave power transmission, phased array transmitter systems, rectenna arrays, large space structure design, in-space assembly, and high-efficiency photovoltaics as primary technology domains. Ground demonstration experiments have tested wireless power beaming at increasing power levels and distances.

Feasibility Boundary

China's SBSP programme has conducted ground demonstrations but has not launched an orbital SBSP demonstration mission as of the knowledge boundary of this reference layer. Published roadmap targets for orbital demonstration represent planning aspirations, not confirmed funded milestones. The gap between ground demonstration and orbital deployment at scale remains the primary feasibility boundary.

Claim Boundary

China's SBSP research represents meaningful technical progress in ground demonstration, but does not represent orbital deployment capability or a near-term operational SBSP system. Roadmap targets are planning references and should not be presented as confirmed commitments. The claim boundary is current ground-demonstration capability, not orbital or operational capability.
