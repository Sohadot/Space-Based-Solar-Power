Airbus Defence and Space has engaged with space-based solar power as a long-range technology concept, conducting internal and externally referenced studies on SBSP system architecture, technology feasibility, and potential development pathways. Airbus's engagement with SBSP reflects the company's role as Europe's primary large spacecraft manufacturer and its strategic interest in emerging space market segments. Airbus SBSP activities occur within the context of ESA SOLARIS and broader European SBSP policy development, contributing industrial perspective to the European SBSP feasibility discourse. Airbus's activities are study and assessment phase, not a development programme for an SBSP product.

Institutional Context

Airbus Defence and Space is Europe's largest space systems company, with major activities in telecommunications satellites, Earth observation, and launch vehicle manufacturing. Airbus has existing capabilities in large satellite platform design, power systems, and GEO spacecraft operations directly relevant to SBSP system concepts. Airbus's engagement with ESA SOLARIS positions it as a key potential industrial partner in any future European SBSP development programme.

SBSP Relevance

Airbus's SBSP engagement is significant as the participation of Europe's primary large spacecraft manufacturer in SBSP feasibility discourse. Airbus's industrial capabilities in large GEO platform design, power systems, and spacecraft manufacturing are directly relevant to SBSP development challenges. Its study-phase engagement helps define the industrial feasibility boundary for European SBSP development.

Technology Relationship

Airbus SBSP studies engage with large space structure dynamics, solar array structural design, spacecraft thermal control, modular spacecraft architecture, and microwave power transmission as primary technology domains within Airbus's existing industrial capability base.

Feasibility Boundary

Airbus SBSP activities are study and assessment phase. No Airbus SBSP product development programme has been confirmed. Industrial capability assessment is distinct from a development commitment.

Claim Boundary

Airbus SBSP engagement should be presented as industrial study and feasibility assessment, not as a commitment to develop or manufacture an SBSP system. Study-phase industrial engagement does not imply a near-term development decision.
