Defense Military

The US Air Force Research Laboratory has conducted space solar power research with a focus on wireless power transmission technology components relevant to both SBSP and defense energy logistics applications. AFRL's SBSP-related research encompasses phased array transmitter development, power conversion, and high-efficiency photovoltaic subsystems. AFRL's engagement with SBSP reflects the US defense establishment's interest in advanced power beaming technologies for both terrestrial and space applications, including forward operating base energy resilience and space-based power relay concepts. AFRL research contributes to the technology component base relevant to future SBSP development.

Institutional Context

AFRL is the primary scientific research organisation of the US Air Force and Space Force, conducting research across aerospace, directed energy, and power systems domains. AFRL's SBSP-relevant research has been conducted in coordination with DARPA programmes and the broader US defense energy research enterprise. The laboratory's work on power beaming and photovoltaic systems has dual-use relevance to both military and civilian SBSP concepts.

SBSP Relevance

AFRL's research on wireless power transmission and photovoltaic subsystems is directly relevant to SBSP technology development. The laboratory's engagement with power beaming at increasing scale and efficiency contributes to the technology readiness base for SBSP microwave transmission subsystems. AFRL also provides an institutional link between SBSP and US defense energy resilience strategy.

Technology Relationship

AFRL SBSP research engages directly with phased array transmitter systems, high-efficiency solar cells, power conversion and conditioning, and microwave power transmission as primary technology domains. AFRL's SSPIDR programme (in partnership with Northrop Grumman) represents the highest-profile US defense-linked SBSP technology demonstration effort.

Feasibility Boundary

AFRL research operates at component and subsystem technology demonstration level. No AFRL-led orbital SBSP system deployment has been planned or funded. Research outputs demonstrate component-level feasibility, not system-level operational capability.

Claim Boundary

AFRL research demonstrates progress in SBSP-relevant technology components but does not demonstrate a complete operational SBSP system. Defense research investment in SBSP-related technologies should not be interpreted as a US military commitment to deploy space-based solar power.
