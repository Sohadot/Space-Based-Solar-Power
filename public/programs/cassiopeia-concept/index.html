CASSIOPeiA (Constant Aperture, Solid-State, Integrated, Orbital Phased Array) is a distinctive SBSP satellite architecture concept developed by International Electric Company and associated with the UK Space Energy Initiative. Unlike conventional SBSP designs using large steerable reflectors or tracking solar arrays, CASSIOPeiA uses a spherical architecture with distributed photovoltaic and microwave transmission elements, enabling constant Earth-pointing without mechanical tracking. CASSIOPeiA represents a significant architectural innovation within SBSP system design studies and has become a reference concept in the international SBSP architecture discourse. It remains a design concept, not a funded development programme.

Institutional Context

CASSIOPeiA was developed by International Electric Company founder Ian Cash and has been adopted as a reference architecture within the UK Space Energy Initiative's technical framework. The concept has been presented at international space conferences and is referenced in the Frazer-Nash feasibility study. Its distinctive non-tracking spherical geometry addresses key mass and complexity challenges in conventional SBSP designs.

SBSP Relevance

CASSIOPeiA is directly relevant as an architectural reference point for understanding the diversity of SBSP system design approaches. Its non-tracking geometry, distributed power electronics, and phased array transmission concept represent a substantively different approach to SBSP system integration compared to conventional designs, and its study-phase analysis has influenced UK and international SBSP feasibility assessments.

Technology Relationship

CASSIOPeiA's architecture concept engages directly with phased array transmitter systems, modular spacecraft architecture, power conversion and conditioning, and solar array structural design as primary technology domains. Its distinctive geometry creates unique requirements for beam steering and distributed power electronics.

Feasibility Boundary

CASSIOPeiA is a design concept at study phase. No funded development programme or orbital demonstration mission has been committed. The concept's feasibility remains subject to analysis of manufacturing, deployment, and in-space operational challenges not yet fully resolved.

Claim Boundary

CASSIOPeiA should be presented as an innovative architectural concept at study phase, not as a validated design ready for development. Its study-phase analysis has produced promising conceptual results, but full feasibility demonstration requires development-phase investment not yet committed.
