The International Telecommunication Union's regulatory framework for spectrum allocation is a critical institutional constraint for any operational space-based solar power system. SBSP microwave wireless power transmission requires allocation of specific frequency bands under international agreement, and the ITU's processes for coordinating spectrum use among nations and services are directly relevant to SBSP deployment planning. ITU discussions on spectrum for space power applications represent an emerging regulatory frontier as national SBSP programmes advance from research to potential demonstration phases. No ITU spectrum allocation specifically for SBSP operational systems has yet been established.

Institutional Context

The ITU is the United Nations specialised agency for information and communication technologies, responsible for coordinating the global use of the radio spectrum and satellite orbits. ITU Radio Regulations govern spectrum allocation and coordination between member states. Any operational SBSP system using microwave wireless power transmission would require ITU-coordinated spectrum allocation in the frequency bands used for power beaming, creating a regulatory pathway that must be engaged well in advance of operational deployment.

SBSP Relevance

ITU spectrum regulation is a non-technical but critical feasibility constraint for operational SBSP. The need to secure internationally coordinated spectrum allocation for microwave power beaming frequencies represents a regulatory hurdle that is independent of technology readiness and must be addressed through ITU processes that operate on long timescales. ITU engagement is therefore a leading indicator of the institutional seriousness of national SBSP programmes.

Technology Relationship

ITU spectrum regulation is most directly relevant to microwave power transmission and phased array transmitter systems as the technology domains whose operational frequency requirements create the regulatory interface with ITU processes.

Feasibility Boundary

No ITU spectrum allocation specifically for SBSP operational power beaming has been established. Spectrum regulatory pathway for SBSP remains to be defined through ITU processes that have not yet been formally initiated for this application.

Claim Boundary

ITU regulatory engagement with SBSP should not be presented as spectrum clearance or regulatory approval for SBSP operations. ITU processes are long-lead regulatory requirements, not endorsements. The absence of established spectrum allocation is a genuine feasibility constraint for operational SBSP deployment.
