Concentrator Photovoltaics in Space Power Systems

Technology Reference

Concentrator photovoltaics (CPV) use optical elements—lenses or mirrors—to focus sunlight onto small, high-efficiency photovoltaic cells, allowing more expensive high-performance cells to cover a smaller area while capturing the energy from a larger optical aperture. In space, CPV systems can potentially achieve higher system efficiencies by combining concentrating optics with multi-junction solar cells capable of 40%+ conversion efficiency under concentrated illumination. For SBSP, CPV offers a potential mass and cost trade-off: the optical concentrator can be lightweight (thin-film reflectors or Fresnel lenses), potentially reducing the total cell area and associated mass versus flat-panel approaches. However, CPV in space introduces thermal management challenges—concentrated solar flux generates intense localized heating—and requires that cells track the focal point precisely, adding pointing complexity. Research programs have explored both refractive and reflective concentrators for space power applications.

Infrastructure Role

Concentrator photovoltaics represent an alternative architecture for the SBSP solar collection subsystem. By reducing the active cell area required per unit of output power, CPV can potentially lower the cost of the photovoltaic component of the satellite while using lightweight optics for concentration. This affects the overall mass budget, structural design, and thermal management requirements of the orbital platform.

Subsystem Relationship

CPV systems are closely coupled to thermal management subsystems due to the high heat flux at cell surfaces under concentration. Optical pointing and tracking systems must maintain alignment between the concentrating element and the cell array. Power conditioning systems must handle the electrical output characteristics of CPV cells, which differ from flat-panel configurations.

Feasibility and Readiness

Terrestrial CPV systems are commercially deployed at TRL 9. Space-adapted CPV with lightweight concentrators has been studied and tested at component level (TRL 4–6) but has not been demonstrated in long-duration orbital operation for SBSP-scale systems. Thermal management at concentration ratios suitable for SBSP remains a research challenge.

Feasibility and Claim Boundary

This page describes concentrator photovoltaics as a technology architecture applicable to SBSP based on published technical literature and terrestrial CPV experience. It does not claim that space CPV systems have been proven at SBSP scale, nor that CPV is the definitive architecture choice over flat-panel approaches. Trade-offs between CPV and flat-panel designs depend on mission-specific parameters not resolved by current research.
