In-Space Manufacturing Systems for SBSP

Technology Reference

In-space manufacturing systems for space-based solar power encompass the concepts, technologies, and facilities that could produce structural components, photovoltaic cells, power electronics, or other satellite elements directly in the space environment—either from raw materials processed from space resources (lunar regolith, asteroidal materials) or from semi-processed feedstocks launched from Earth. In-space manufacturing is proposed as a long-term strategy to reduce the mass that must be launched from Earth's gravity well, potentially enabling SBSP at scales otherwise prohibitive due to launch costs. Manufacturing in the space environment offers some potential advantages—microgravity, vacuum, and abundant solar energy—but also imposes severe challenges: remote operation, radiation exposure, extreme temperature cycling, and the absence of established manufacturing infrastructure. Research areas include additive manufacturing in microgravity, in-space solar cell deposition, automated material processing, and utilization of lunar or asteroidal raw materials. Most in-space manufacturing concepts for SBSP are at very early technology readiness levels.

Infrastructure Role

In-space manufacturing represents a potential long-term pathway to reduce the dominant SBSP cost constraint of launch economics by producing satellite components in orbit from space-derived materials. If mature, it could fundamentally change the economics of large-scale SBSP deployment by decoupling production scale from Earth's launch capacity. However, it remains a speculative long-term enabler rather than a near-term operational capability.

Subsystem Relationship

In-space manufacturing systems would interface with the launch and logistics subsystem by reducing the required manifest of Earth-launched components. They interact with robotic operations and assembly systems, which would use manufactured components. Orbital resource utilization for manufacturing depends on cislunar logistics infrastructure and resource extraction systems currently in early development.

Feasibility and Readiness

Additive manufacturing in microgravity has been demonstrated at small scale in laboratory settings and on the ISS (TRL 5–6 for specific processes). In-space manufacturing of photovoltaic cells, precision power electronics, or large structural components has not been demonstrated (TRL 1–3). In-space manufacturing at the scale needed for SBSP is considered a multi-decade research and development challenge with fundamental questions unresolved.

Feasibility and Claim Boundary

This page describes in-space manufacturing systems as a studied long-term enabler for SBSP based on published research and technology roadmaps. It does not claim that in-space manufacturing at SBSP-relevant scale or capability has been demonstrated, or that space-resource-derived components of sufficient quality for SBSP applications can be produced with current or near-term technology. This technology domain carries the highest uncertainty in the SBSP technology portfolio.
