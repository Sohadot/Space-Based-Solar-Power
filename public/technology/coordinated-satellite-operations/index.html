Coordinated Satellite Operations for SBSP

Technology Reference

Coordinated satellite operations for space-based solar power refers to the command, control, and coordination systems and protocols that manage the operational behavior of one or more SBSP satellites, their interaction with ground receiving stations, the power grid, safety systems, and regulatory oversight bodies. At system level, coordinated operations encompass beam pointing control to maintain accurate microwave power delivery to designated rectennas, automatic beam shutdown and retargeting in response to safety system alerts, load-following adjustments to match power output to grid demand, coordination between multiple SBSP satellites that may serve the same or different ground stations, and communication with ground control infrastructure. Long propagation delays to GEO (~0.24 s one-way) limit the responsiveness of ground-commanded control and drive the need for onboard autonomous operations with supervisory oversight from ground stations. Operational coordination also extends to regulatory compliance—spectrum use, interference avoidance with other GEO satellites, and safety zone enforcement—requiring interfaces with national and international regulatory authorities.

Infrastructure Role

Coordinated satellite operations are the operational management layer that makes SBSP a controllable, reliable power source rather than an uncontrolled energy emitter. Operations systems determine the safety, grid integration behavior, and regulatory compliance of the SBSP satellite constellation. Without robust operations coordination, SBSP cannot be safely integrated into the terrestrial energy infrastructure or approved by regulatory bodies.

Subsystem Relationship

Operations coordination interfaces with the beam steering and phased-array transmitter subsystem for pointing control, with safety monitoring systems for emergency response, with the grid operator for demand-following, with fault detection and robotic servicing systems for maintenance coordination, and with regulatory bodies for spectrum and safety compliance. Communication architecture must support both routine operations telemetry and safety-critical command uplinks.

Feasibility and Readiness

Satellite operations management is at TRL 9 for conventional GEO communications satellites. The specific requirements of SBSP operations—real-time beam safety monitoring and shutdown, grid-coupled power delivery coordination, multi-satellite management at power-generation scale—have no direct precedent. Regulatory frameworks for SBSP operations do not currently exist, and their development represents a parallel requirement to technical readiness.

Feasibility and Claim Boundary

This page describes coordinated satellite operations requirements for SBSP based on existing satellite operations practice and published SBSP system studies. It does not claim that SBSP operations frameworks have been designed, approved, or demonstrated. Regulatory requirements for beam safety monitoring, spectrum use, and grid integration of SBSP are not established, and the timeline for their development is uncertain.
