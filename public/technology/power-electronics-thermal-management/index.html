Power Electronics Thermal Management in SBSP

Technology Reference

Power electronics thermal management in space-based solar power satellites addresses the specific challenge of maintaining power conversion and conditioning circuits—DC-DC converters, inverters, power amplifiers, and control electronics—within their safe operating temperature ranges while minimizing the mass of the thermal management system required to do so. Power electronics in SBSP operate at very high aggregate power levels (potentially gigawatts), and even small inefficiencies at each stage produce large absolute waste heat loads that must be rejected to space. Unlike terrestrial power electronics that can use convective air or liquid cooling, space power electronics must rely entirely on conduction to heat spreaders and radiative rejection via radiator panels. Wide-bandgap semiconductors (SiC, GaN) offer higher thermal conductivity and higher operating temperature capability than silicon, enabling power electronics to operate at elevated temperatures and thereby improve the efficiency of radiative heat rejection (which scales with temperature to the fourth power). Junction temperature management, thermal interface materials, and layout optimization are critical design variables for space power electronics.

Infrastructure Role

Power electronics thermal management is integral to the power conversion and conditioning subsystem of both the satellite and the ground station. It determines the maximum power density achievable in space power electronics, the achievable conversion efficiency at operating temperatures, and the mass overhead required for thermal control of the power electronics components. Failures in power electronics thermal management are a primary reliability risk for SBSP systems.

Subsystem Relationship

Power electronics thermal management is tightly coupled to heat pipe and radiator systems, which provide the ultimate heat rejection pathway. Component placement, heat spreader design, and thermal interface material selection are determined jointly by electronic performance requirements and thermal constraints. Satellite structural design must accommodate both the electronic components and their thermal management hardware.

Feasibility and Readiness

Power electronics thermal management is at TRL 9 for conventional satellite electronics at current power levels. Space-rated wide-bandgap power electronics with optimized thermal management for SBSP-level power densities are at TRL 5–7. Achieving the combination of high efficiency, high power density, low specific mass, and long operational lifetime in the GEO radiation environment simultaneously has not been demonstrated.

Feasibility and Claim Boundary

This page documents power electronics thermal management for SBSP based on established power electronics thermal engineering and published space electronics research. It does not claim that wide-bandgap power electronics at SBSP power levels with the required space qualification have been achieved, or that the specific mass of space power electronics will reach the targets projected in SBSP conceptual studies. Thermal performance of power electronics in the GEO radiation environment over multi-decade lifetimes requires extended testing not yet performed.
