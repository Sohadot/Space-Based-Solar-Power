Ground Receiving Infrastructure for SBSP

Technology Reference

Ground receiving infrastructure for space-based solar power encompasses the complete terrestrial installation required to accept wirelessly transmitted power from an orbital satellite, convert it to grid-compatible electricity, and deliver it to the electrical distribution network. This infrastructure includes the rectenna field or laser photovoltaic receiver array, power conditioning equipment, substation infrastructure, grid interconnection, exclusion zone management systems, and monitoring and control facilities. Unlike conventional power plants, SBSP ground infrastructure has a large land footprint—proposals for gigawatt-class systems suggest rectenna fields of 5–10 km² or larger—but the land within the rectenna field can in some designs be used for lower-height agriculture or other compatible land uses since the beam passes through rather than shadowing the area. Site selection for the ground station requires analysis of land availability, grid proximity, electromagnetic compatibility with adjacent spectrum users, regulatory constraints, and safety buffer zone requirements. Siting near existing grid infrastructure reduces transmission infrastructure costs.

Infrastructure Role

The ground receiving infrastructure is the terrestrial interface of the SBSP system, connecting orbital power generation to the national or regional electricity grid. Its capacity, siting, and grid integration determine how SBSP power enters the energy system and at what voltage and grid service characteristics. Infrastructure cost and permitting complexity are significant factors in overall SBSP economics.

Subsystem Relationship

Ground receiving infrastructure is downstream of the wireless power transmission subsystem and feeds into the power conversion and conditioning systems. Grid interconnection design must account for the characteristics of the DC-to-AC conversion process and the power output profile of the SBSP satellite. Safety and regulatory monitoring systems for beam arrival confirmation and emergency shutdown are integrated into the receiving station.

Feasibility and Readiness

Individual components of SBSP ground infrastructure (substations, grid interconnection, monitoring) are at TRL 9. The specific challenge of large-scale rectenna field construction, siting approval for multi-km² exclusion zones, and regulatory licensing for microwave energy reception at SBSP power levels has no precedent. Land acquisition and regulatory permitting for SBSP ground stations are uncharacterized lead-time and cost risks.

Feasibility and Claim Boundary

This page describes the ground receiving infrastructure requirements for SBSP based on published system studies and existing electrical infrastructure technologies. It does not claim that siting, permitting, or construction of full-scale SBSP ground receiving stations has been accomplished or that the regulatory pathway is established. Cost and timeline projections carry high uncertainty given the absence of precedent projects.
