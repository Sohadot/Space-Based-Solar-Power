Large Space Structure Dynamics for SBSP

Technology Reference

Large space structure dynamics is the branch of orbital mechanics and structural engineering concerned with the vibrational, rotational, and deformation behavior of very large spacecraft or orbital structures in the space environment. For space-based solar power satellites, which must be several kilometers in extent, the structural dynamics problem is qualitatively different from that of conventional satellites. At kilometer scale, structures become highly flexible and exhibit very low-frequency vibration modes—natural frequencies that may fall below 0.01 Hz—that can interact with attitude control systems, cause resonant amplification from periodic disturbances, and affect the pointing accuracy of both the solar collection array and the microwave transmitter. Environmental disturbances include solar radiation pressure, gravitational gradient torques, thermal snap from orbital dawn/dusk transitions, and micrometeorite impacts. Control of large flexible structures in orbit requires either passive structural damping, active vibration control systems, or attitude control algorithms that avoid exciting low-frequency modes. Predicting these dynamics accurately requires validated models of large flexible structures in the relevant environment.

Infrastructure Role

Understanding and controlling large space structure dynamics is essential for the operational stability of the SBSP satellite. Structural vibrations affect solar array pointing toward the sun and transmitter beam pointing toward the ground rectenna. Uncontrolled flexible dynamics can degrade power generation efficiency, transmission accuracy, and structural integrity over the satellite's operational lifetime.

Subsystem Relationship

Structural dynamics is closely coupled to attitude control and beam pointing systems, which must compensate for structural flexibility. Thermal management affects structural dynamics through differential thermal expansion. The structural design of the satellite must be optimized to minimize dynamic coupling between structural modes and control system bandwidth. In-space assembly methods affect the structural integrity and modal properties of the final assembled configuration.

Feasibility and Readiness

Flexible spacecraft dynamics analysis is well-established analytically (TRL 9 for modeling). Control of large flexible structures has been partially demonstrated on ISS (TRL 7 for human-operated systems). The specific challenge of autonomous structural dynamics control for SBSP-scale structures at GEO, over multi-decade operation without crew, is at TRL 3–5. Experimental validation of models at SBSP scale does not exist.

Feasibility and Claim Boundary

This page describes large space structure dynamics relevant to SBSP based on established structural mechanics, astrodynamics literature, and published SBSP studies. It does not claim that the structural dynamics of specific SBSP designs have been experimentally verified or that control solutions for SBSP-scale flexible structures have been demonstrated. Analytical predictions carry uncertainty that increases significantly with structure size.
