# Multilingual Policy: Space-Based-Solar-Power.com

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the multilingual architecture of Space-Based-Solar-Power.com.

The asset is multilingual because SBSP is not a single-market topic. It connects energy sovereignty, space infrastructure, industrial policy, defense resilience, artificial intelligence, and future orbital economics across multiple power blocs.

## 2. Source Language

English is the source-of-truth language.

All primary content, source boundaries, canonical concepts, strategic tools, and metadata models must originate in English unless a future documented decision changes this rule.

## 3. Language Editions

The approved language hierarchy is:

1. English `/`
2. Arabic `/ar/`
3. Chinese `/zh/`
4. Japanese `/ja/`
5. French `/fr/`
6. German `/de/`
7. Spanish `/es/`

Additional languages require a documented decision.

## 4. Localization Philosophy

Localization is not raw translation.

Each language edition must preserve the core thesis while adapting the strategic emphasis to its audience.

The approved strategic framing is:

- English: institutional source layer.
- Arabic: energy sovereignty and post-hydrocarbon transition.
- Chinese: industrial scale and strategic infrastructure execution.
- Japanese: precision engineering and SSPS lineage.
- French: European aerospace policy and strategic autonomy.
- German: engineering discipline, grid integration, and energy transition.
- Spanish: global expansion and public accessibility.

## 5. Production Rule

No localized page may enter production unless it is:

- complete;
- reviewed;
- source-disciplined;
- internally linked;
- metadata-complete;
- hreflang-valid;
- canonical-valid;
- free of placeholder content.

Draft, incomplete, raw machine translated, or unreviewed localized pages must remain unpublished.

## 6. URL Policy

Language directories partition localized content.

Core slugs remain standardized to the English source layer.

Example:

- `/what-is-space-based-solar-power/`
- `/ar/what-is-space-based-solar-power/`
- `/zh/what-is-space-based-solar-power/`
- `/ja/what-is-space-based-solar-power/`
- `/fr/what-is-space-based-solar-power/`
- `/de/what-is-space-based-solar-power/`
- `/es/what-is-space-based-solar-power/`

Localized slugs are not allowed unless a future documented decision changes the URL architecture.

## 7. Hreflang Policy

Hreflang must include only published and validated language variants for the same content node.

Hreflang must not point to:

- drafts;
- missing pages;
- placeholders;
- redirects;
- unpublished language variants;
- machine-translated review pages.

Each localized variant must use a self-referencing canonical URL.

## 8. RTL Policy

Arabic pages must use:

`lang="ar"` and `dir="rtl"`.

The design system must support right-to-left layout without breaking navigation, spacing, typography, or internal linking.

## 9. Language Switcher Policy

The language switcher must not link to unavailable variants.

If a page exists in English and Arabic only, the switcher for that page must show only English and Arabic.

No language switcher may point to a 404 page.

## 10. Sitemap Policy

Only published and validated localized pages may appear in the sitemap.

Drafts, placeholders, and unreviewed variants must be excluded.

## 11. Quality Requirement

The multilingual system must strengthen the asset’s authority.

It must never create thin pages, duplicated low-value content, broken language journeys, or random international SEO expansion.
