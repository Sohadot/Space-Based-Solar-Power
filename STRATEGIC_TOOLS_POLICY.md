# Strategic Tools Policy: Space-Based-Solar-Power.com

## 1. Purpose

This policy governs the strategic tools layer of Space-Based-Solar-Power.com.

The tools are not entertainment widgets, speculative calculators, or traffic gimmicks. They are governed reference instruments designed to help serious audiences reason about Space-Based Solar Power and Orbital Energy Infrastructure.

## 2. Approved Audience Classes

Strategic tools may serve:

- researchers;
- specialized space and energy companies;
- journalists;
- investors;
- governments;
- public-sector observers;
- energy strategists;
- space infrastructure analysts.

## 3. Core Tool Principle

The tools do not predict the future of space-based solar power.

They make the strategic structure of the category visible.

## 4. Approved Initial Tool Classes

The first approved tool layer prioritizes qualitative and source-disciplined frameworks.

Approved initial tools include:

- Orbital Energy Constraint Matrix;
- SBSP Glossary Navigator;
- Global Program Tracker;
- Use-Case Fit Evaluator;
- Claim Boundary Checker;
- Journalist Brief Builder;
- Investor Due Diligence Checklist;
- Government Strategic Fit Matrix;
- Orbital Energy Readiness Index.

## 5. Numerical Tool Restriction

Numerical calculators and scenario models are prohibited until a dedicated methodology is approved.

Future numerical tools may include:

- launch cost sensitivity explorer;
- rectenna footprint scenario model;
- wireless power transmission pathway model;
- orbital power delivery scenario model.

These tools require:

- methodology documentation;
- source ranges;
- assumption disclosure;
- versioning;
- limitation statements;
- quality-gate validation.

## 6. No Financial, Investment, or Policy Advice

Tools must not provide financial advice, investment recommendations, legal advice, procurement recommendations, or government policy instructions.

Tools may provide structured questions, classification logic, constraint maps, and due-diligence frameworks.

## 7. Output Integrity

Every tool output must avoid:

- false precision;
- unsupported readiness scores;
- exaggerated feasibility;
- hidden assumptions;
- single-number certainty;
- commercial hype;
- investment signals.

Where uncertainty exists, the tool must show uncertainty.

## 8. Source Linkage

Every tool must be linked to:

- relevant source entries;
- methodology notes;
- related reference pages;
- limitation statements.

## 9. Publication Gate

No strategic tool may be published unless it is:

- complete;
- tested;
- source-disciplined;
- internally linked;
- metadata-complete;
- accessible;
- responsive;
- free of broken outputs;
- approved under the quality gate.
